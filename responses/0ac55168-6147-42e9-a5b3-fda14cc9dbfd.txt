\[
\sigma = \frac{R_1 + R_2}{R_1 - R_2}
\]

Given \( R_1 = +15 \, \text{cm} \) and \( R_2 = -30 \, \text{cm} \), we substitute:

\[
\sigma = \frac{15 + (-30)}{15 - (-30)} = \frac{-15}{45} = -\frac{1}{3}
\]

Thus, \( \sigma = -\frac{1}{3} \).

For the position factor, \( \pi \), the formula is:

\[
\pi = (1 + \sigma) \cdot \frac{d}{f}
\]

\[
\frac{1}{f} = (n - 1) \left( \frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2} \right)
\]

Substituting \( n = 1.60 \), \( R_1 = +15 \, \text{cm} \), and \( R_2 = -30 \, \text{cm} \):

\[
\frac{1}{f} = 0.60 \left( \frac{1}{15} - \frac{1}{-30} \right) = 0.60 \left( \frac{1}{15} + \frac{1}{30} \right) = 0.60 \cdot \frac{1}{10} = 0.06

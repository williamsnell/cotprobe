The solution contains 1 mg/ml of myosin and 10^14 latex particles/ml. When a sample is viewed, each field contains 122 protein molecules and 10 latex particles.

\[
\text{Mass of myosin} = 1 \, \text{mg/ml} \times 1e-13 \, \text{ml} = 1e-13 \, \text{mg} = 1e-16 \, \text{g}.
\]

Since this mass corresponds to 122 myosin molecules, the mass per molecule is:

\[
\text{Mass per molecule} = \frac{1e-16 \, \text{g}}{122} \approx 8.1967e-19 \, \text{g/molecule}.
\]

The molecular weight is then determined by multiplying the mass per molecule by Avogadro's number (6.022e23 molecules/mol):

\[
\text{Molecular weight} = 8.1967e-19 \, \text{g/molecule} \times 6.022e23 \, \text{molecules/mol} \approx 4.936e5 \, \text{g/mol}.
\]

To determine the number of men needed to build the boat in 77 days, we can use the principle that the total amount of work (man-days) remains constant. 

Given:
- 36 men take 132 days to build the boat.
- We need to find the number of men (x) required to build the same boat in 77 days.

The total work is:
\[ 36 \text{ men} \times 132 \text{ days} = 4752 \text{ man-days} \]

For 77 days, the number of men needed is:
\[ x = \frac{4752 \text{ man-days}}{77 \text{ days}} \]

Calculating:
\[ x = \frac{4752}{77} = 62 \]

To solve this, we analyze the energy transformations. The mate imparts initial kinetic energy (KE_initial) to the brick, which is converted into both kinetic energy (KE_final) and potential energy (PE) at the bricklayer's level. The energy lost, which serves no useful purpose, is the difference between the initial kinetic energy and the sum of the final kinetic and potential energies.

1. **Initial Kinetic Energy (KE_initial):**  
   Using the kinematic equation \( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2as \), where \( v = 6 \, \text{ft/s} \), \( a = -32.174 \, \text{ft/s}^2 \), and \( s = 10 \, \text{ft} \), we calculate the initial velocity \( v_0 \):  
   \[
   6^2 = v_0^2 + 2(-32.174)(10) \implies v_0^2 = 679.48 \implies v_0 \approx 26.07 \, \text{ft/s}.
   \]  
   The initial kinetic energy is then:  
   \[
   KE_{\text{initial}} = \frac{1}{2} m v_0^2 = \frac{1}{2} m (26.07)^2 \approx 339.74 m.
   \]

2. **Final Kinetic Energy (KE_final) and Potential Energy (PE):**  
   At the bricklayer's level, the kinetic energy is:  
   \[
   KE_{\text{final}} = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 = \frac{1}{2} m (6)^2 = 18 m.
   \]  
   The potential energy gained is:  
   \[
   PE = m g h = m (32.174)(10) \approx 321.74 m.
   \]  
   The total useful energy is:  
   \[
   KE_{\text{final}} + PE = 18 m + 321.74 m = 339.74 m.
   \]

3. **Energy Lost:**  
   The energy lost is the difference between the initial kinetic energy and the total useful energy:  
   \[
   \text{Energy lost} = KE_{\text{initial}} - (KE_{\text{final}} + PE) = 339.74 m - 339.74 m = 18 m.
   \]

4. **Percentage of Energy Lost:**  
   The percentage of energy lost is:  
   \[
   \frac{\text{Energy lost}}{KE_{\text{initial}}} \times 100 = \frac{18 m}{339.74 m} \times 100 \approx 5.3\%.
   \]

Thus, the percentage of energy used by the mate that serves no useful purpose is **5.3%**.

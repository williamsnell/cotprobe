\[
n\lambda = 2d\sin\theta
\]  
where \(n = 1\), \(\lambda\) is the wavelength, \(d = 77.8 \, \text{pm}\) is the interplanar spacing, and \(\theta\) is the glancing angle. Rearranging for \(\sin\theta\):  
\[
\sin\theta = \frac{\lambda}{2d}
\]  
The glancing angle \(2\theta\) is then calculated as \(2 \times \arcsin\left(\frac{\lambda}{2d}\right)\).  

### Step 1: Calculate \(2\theta\) for each wavelength
For \(\lambda_1 = 154.433 \, \text{pm}\):  
\[
\sin\theta_1 = \frac{154.433}{2 \times 77.8} = \frac{154.433}{155.6} \approx 0.99248

\[
\theta_1 \approx \arcsin(0.99248) \approx 81.8^\circ

2\theta_1 \approx 2 \times 81.8^\circ = 163.6^\circ

\theta_2 \approx \arcsin(0.9900) \approx 81.6^\circ

2\theta_2 \approx 2 \times 81.6^\circ = 163.2^\circ

The efficiency of a reversible steam engine is determined using the Carnot formula: 

\[
\eta = 1 - \frac{T_{\text{cold}}}{T_{\text{hot}}}
\]

where \( T_{\text{hot}} \) and \( T_{\text{cold}} \) are the absolute temperatures of the hot and cold reservoirs in Kelvin. The secondary reservoir is given at 35°C, which converts to 308 K. Assuming the primary reservoir (hot reservoir) is at a typical temperature for steam engines, such as 100°C (373 K), the efficiency can be calculated:

\[
\eta = 1 - \frac{308}{373} \approx 1 - 0.825 = 0.175 \, \text{or} \, 17.5\%
\]

This corresponds closely to option B (17.4%).

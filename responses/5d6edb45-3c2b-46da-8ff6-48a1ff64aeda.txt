The Poynting vector represents the power per unit area carried by an electromagnetic wave and is related to the electric field (E) and magnetic field (H). For a plane wave in a medium, the average Poynting vector (intensity) is given by:

\[
I = \frac{E_0^2}{2 \eta}
\]

where \( E_0 \) is the amplitude of the electric field, and \( \eta \) is the intrinsic impedance of the medium. The intrinsic impedance \( \eta \) is calculated as:

\[
\eta = \frac{\eta_0}{n}
\]

where \( \eta_0 \approx 377 \, \Omega \) is the impedance of free space, and \( n = 1.5 \) is the refractive index of glass. Substituting \( n = 1.5 \), we find:

\[
\eta = \frac{377}{1.5} \approx 251.33 \, \Omega
\]

The electric field amplitude is given as \( E_0 = 100 \, \text{V/m} \). Substituting into the formula for intensity:

\[
I = \frac{100^2}{2 \times 251.33} = \frac{10,000}{502.66} \approx 19.9 \, \text{W/m}^2
\]

Thus, the magnitude of the Poynting vector (average intensity) is approximately \( 19.9 \, \text{W/m}^2 \), corresponding to option A.

To determine the focal length of the lens in the slide projector, we use the thin lens formula and the magnification relationship. The slide projector projects a 40-inch by 60-inch image onto a screen 10 feet (120 inches) away. The slide size is 35 mm, which corresponds to an object height of 35 mm (1.378 inches) when considering the width of the slide.

### Key Equations:
1. **Magnification**:  
   \[
   M = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = \frac{d_i}{d_o}
   \]
   where \( h_i \) is the image height, \( h_o \) is the object height, \( d_i \) is the image distance, and \( d_o \) is the object distance.

2. **Thin Lens Formula**:  
   \[
   \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}
   \]

### Calculations:
1. **Magnification**:  
   Using \( h_o = 35 \, \text{mm} = 1.378 \, \text{inches} \) and \( h_i = 60 \, \text{inches} \):  
   \[
   M = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = \frac{60}{1.378} \approx 43.53
   \]

2. **Object Distance**:  
   From \( M = \frac{d_i}{d_o} \):  
   \[
   d_o = \frac{d_i}{M} = \frac{120}{43.53} \approx 2.756 \, \text{inches}
   \]

3. **Focal Length**:  
   Substituting \( d_o = 2.756 \, \text{inches} \) and \( d_i = 120 \, \text{inches} \) into the thin lens formula:  
   \[
   \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{2.756} + \frac{1}{120} \approx 0.3625 + 0.00833 \approx 0.3708
   \]  
   \[
   f \approx \frac{1}{0.3708} \approx 2.697 \, \text{inches}
   \]

The focal length of the lens is approximately **2.69 inches**, which corresponds to **option D**.

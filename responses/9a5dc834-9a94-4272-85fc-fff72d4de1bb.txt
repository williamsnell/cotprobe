The problem involves determining the exit temperature of water after passing through a reversible, adiabatic pump. The inlet conditions are 15 kPa and 40°C, and the outlet pressure is 5 MPa. Since the process is adiabatic and reversible, entropy remains constant, i.e., \( s_1 = s_2 \). 

At the inlet, the water is a compressed liquid because the temperature (40°C) is below the saturation temperature at 15 kPa (approximately 52.8°C). Thus, the entropy at the inlet is \( s_1 = s_f(40^\circ \text{C}) \approx 1.5301 \, \text{kJ/kg·K} \).

At the exit, the pressure is 5 MPa, and the entropy remains \( s_2 = s_1 = 1.5301 \, \text{kJ/kg·K} \). Since this entropy value corresponds to the saturated liquid entropy at 40°C, the water remains a compressed liquid at 40°C. However, this contradicts the expectation that the pump increases the temperature due to work input.

Using the relation for entropy change in a reversible adiabatic process:

\[
\ln\left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right) = \frac{R}{C_p} \ln\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right)
\]

where \( R = 0.0821 \, \text{kJ/kg·K} \) and \( C_p = 4.18 \, \text{kJ/kg·K} \), we calculate:

\[
\ln\left(\frac{T_2}{40}\right) = \frac{0.0821}{4.18} \ln\left(\frac{5000}{15}\right) \approx 0.01964 \cdot 5.809 \approx 0.1143
\]

Exponentiating:

\[
\frac{T_2}{40} \approx e^{0.1143} \approx 1.121 \quad \Rightarrow \quad T_2 \approx 40 \cdot 1.121 \approx 44.84^\circ \text{C}
\]

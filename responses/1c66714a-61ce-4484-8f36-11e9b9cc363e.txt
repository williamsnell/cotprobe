The problem involves calculating the average cutting force exerted by a flywheel in a cutting machine. The flywheel's rotational kinetic energy decreases as it performs work during the cutting stroke. By determining the change in kinetic energy and relating it to the work done, the average force can be found.

1. **Moment of Inertia (I):**  
   Using the radius of gyration \( k = 3 \, \text{ft} \) and mass \( m = 62.5 \, \text{slugs} \),  
   \[
   I = k^2 \cdot m = 3^2 \cdot 62.5 = 562.5 \, \text{slug-ft}^2
   \]

2. **Angular Velocities (ω):**  
   Converting rotational speeds from RPM to rad/s:  
   \[
   \omega_{\text{initial}} = 120 \cdot \frac{2\pi}{60} = 4\pi \, \text{rad/s}
   \]  
   \[
   \omega_{\text{final}} = 90 \cdot \frac{2\pi}{60} = 3\pi \, \text{rad/s}
   \]

3. **Kinetic Energy (KE):**  
   Initial KE:  
   \[
   KE_{\text{initial}} = \frac{1}{2} I \omega_{\text{initial}}^2 = 0.5 \cdot 562.5 \cdot (4\pi)^2 = 44,413.2 \, \text{ft-lbf}
   \]  
   Final KE:  
   \[
   KE_{\text{final}} = \frac{1}{2} I \omega_{\text{final}}^2 = 0.5 \cdot 562.5 \cdot (3\pi)^2 = 24,982.6 \, \text{ft-lbf}
   \]  
   Change in KE:  
   \[
   \Delta KE = KE_{\text{initial}} - KE_{\text{final}} = 44,413.2 - 24,982.6 = 19,430.6 \, \text{ft-lbf}
   \]

4. **Work Done and Force:**  
   The work done is equal to the change in KE, \( W = \Delta KE = 19,430.6 \, \text{ft-lbf} \).  
   The stroke length is \( 6 \, \text{in} = 0.5 \, \text{ft} \).  
   The average force is:  
   \[
   F = \frac{W}{\text{stroke}} = \frac{19,430.6}{0.5} = 38,861.2 \, \text{lb}
   \]

The closest option is **A: 38,861.6 lb**.

\[
E_n = \frac{n^2 h^2}{8 m L^2}
\]

where:
- \( h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J·s} \) (Planck's constant),
- \( m = 9.109 \times 10^{-31} \, \text{kg} \) (mass of the electron),
- \( L = 0.2 \, \text{nm} = 2 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{m} \) (width of the box).

\[
E_1 = \frac{h^2}{8 m L^2}, \quad E_3 = \frac{9 h^2}{8 m L^2}
\]

\[
\Delta E_{\text{excite}} = E_3 - E_1 = \frac{8 h^2}{8 m L^2} = \frac{h^2}{m L^2}
\]

\[
\Delta E_{\text{excite}} = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})^2}{(9.109 \times 10^{-31})(2 \times 10^{-10})^2}

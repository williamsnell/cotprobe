When the string is taut, the books are connected, and the forces acting on them are gravity and tension in the string. The falling book experiences a net force due to gravity and tension, while the book on the table is pulled by the tension in the string. Since there is no friction, the only forces are gravitational and tensile.

\[
a = \frac{m_1 g}{m_1 + m_2}
\]

where \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) are the masses of the falling and stationary books, respectively. This acceleration is always less than \(g\) because \(m_1 + m_2 > m_1\). Additionally, since both masses are positive, the acceleration is greater than zero. Therefore, the acceleration of the books is a value between zero and \(g\).

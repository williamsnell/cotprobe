The frequency \( f \) in Hz is given by:  
\[
f = \frac{\text{rpm} \times p}{120} = \frac{1200 \times 6}{120} = 60 \, \text{Hz}.
\]

The average emf for a rotating coil is:  
\[
E_{\text{av}} = N \cdot p \cdot \phi \cdot f.

\[
E_{\text{av}} = 5 \cdot 6 \cdot 3 \times 10^{-2} \cdot 60 = 54 \, \text{V}.
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the Amplitude of the Emf (\( E_m \))
The amplitude (peak value) of the emf is:  
\[
E_m = E_{\text{av}} \cdot \frac{\pi}{2}.
\]

\[
E_m = 54 \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} \approx 54 \cdot 1.5708 \approx 84.39 \, \text{V}.

\[
E_{\text{rms}} = \frac{E_m}{\sqrt{2}}.

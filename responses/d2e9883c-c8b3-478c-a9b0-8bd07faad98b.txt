Raoult's Law states that the partial pressure of each component in the vapor phase is proportional to its mole fraction in the liquid phase and its pure component vapor pressure: 

\[
P = x \cdot P^\circ
\]

where \(P\) is the partial pressure, \(x\) is the mole fraction in the liquid, and \(P^\circ\) is the vapor pressure of the pure component.

### Key Observations:
1. **Total Pressure Constraint**: The total pressure is given as 1.5 atm, so the sum of the partial pressures of benzene (\(P_B\)) and iso-pentane (\(P_P\)) must equal 1.5 atm:
   \[
   P_B + P_P = 1.5 \, \text{atm}
   \]

2. **Mole Fraction Relationship**: The mole fractions in the liquid phase are related as:
   \[
   x_B + x_P = 1
   \]

3. **Vapor Compositions**: The mole fractions in the vapor phase (\(y_B, y_P\)) are determined by the ratio of the partial pressures:
   \[
   y_B = \frac{P_B}{P_B + P_P}, \quad y_P = \frac{P_P}{P_B + P_P}

The Boyle temperature \( T_B \) of a van der Waals gas is the temperature at which the second virial coefficient \( B \) becomes zero. For the van der Waals equation, the second virial coefficient is given by \( B = 2b - \frac{a}{RT} \). Setting \( B = 0 \), we solve:

\[
2b - \frac{a}{RT_B} = 0 \implies \frac{a}{RT_B} = 2b \implies T_B = \frac{a}{2bR}.
\]

This matches option A. The units of \( T_B \) are consistent, as \( a \) has units of \( \text{L}^2 \cdot \text{atm}/\text{mol}^2 \), \( b \) has units of \( \text{L}/\text{mol} \), and \( R \) has units of \( \text{L} \cdot \text{atm}/(\text{mol} \cdot \text{K}) \), ensuring that \( T_B \) has units of \( \text{K} \).

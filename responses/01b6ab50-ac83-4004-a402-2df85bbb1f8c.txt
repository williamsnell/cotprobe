To calculate the compression factor \( Z \) for water vapor at \( P = 327.6 \, \mathrm{atm} \) and \( T = 776.4 \, \mathrm{K} \), we use the formula:

\[
Z = \frac{V_{\text{real}}}{V_{\text{ideal}}}
\]

### Step 1: Calculate \( V_{\text{real}} \)
The mass density \( \rho \) is given as \( 133.2 \, \mathrm{kg/m^3} \). For a volume \( V = 1 \, \mathrm{m^3} \), the mass \( m \) is:

\[
m = \rho \cdot V = 133.2 \, \mathrm{kg}
\]

The molar mass \( M \) of water is \( 18.02 \, \mathrm{g/mol} = 0.01802 \, \mathrm{kg/mol} \). The number of moles \( n \) is:

\[
n = \frac{m}{M} = \frac{133.2}{0.01802} \approx 7399.8 \, \mathrm{mol}
\]

The molar volume of the real gas \( V_{\text{real}} \) is:

\[
V_{\text{real}} = \frac{V}{n} = \frac{1 \, \mathrm{m^3}}{7399.8 \, \mathrm{mol}} \approx 1.351 \times 10^{-4} \, \mathrm{m^3/mol}
\]

Convert \( V_{\text{real}} \) to \( \mathrm{dm^3/mol} \) (\( 1 \, \mathrm{m^3} = 1000 \, \mathrm{dm^3} \)):

\[
V_{\text{real}} \approx 0.1351 \, \mathrm{dm^3/mol}
\]

### Step 2: Calculate \( V_{\text{ideal}} \)
Using the ideal gas law, \( V_{\text{ideal}} = \frac{RT}{P} \), where \( R = 0.0821 \, \mathrm{L \cdot atm/(mol \cdot K)} \), \( T = 776.4 \, \mathrm{K} \), and \( P = 327.6 \, \mathrm{atm} \):

\[
V_{\text{ideal}} = \frac{0.0821 \cdot 776.4}{327.6} \approx 0.1946 \, \mathrm{dm^3/mol}
\]

### Step 3: Calculate \( Z \)
The compression factor is:

\[
Z = \frac{V_{\text{real}}}{V_{\text{ideal}}} = \frac{0.1351}{0.1946} \approx 0.693

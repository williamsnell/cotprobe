To calculate the molecular weight of serum globulin, the Stokes-Einstein equation is used, which relates the diffusion coefficient \( D \) to the molecular weight \( M \). The equation is:

\[
D = \frac{k \cdot T}{6 \pi \eta r}
\]

where \( k \) is Boltzmann's constant, \( T \) is the temperature, \( \eta \) is the viscosity of water, and \( r \) is the radius of the protein molecule. The radius \( r \) can be expressed in terms of the molecular weight \( M \) using the specific volume \( \rho \) (volume per unit mass) and the volume of a sphere:

\[

This occurs when the sum of the partial pressures of the components equals the total pressure.

Using Raoult's Law, the partial pressure of each component is given by:

\[
P_i = x_i \cdot P_{\text{pure},i}(T)
\]

where \( x_i \) is the mole fraction and \( P_{\text{pure},i}(T) \) is the vapor pressure of the pure component at temperature \( T \). The total pressure is the sum of the partial pressures:

\[
P_{\text{total}} = P_{\text{benzene}} + P_{\text{n-pentane}}
\]

\[

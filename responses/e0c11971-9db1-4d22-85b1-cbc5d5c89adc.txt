To determine the size of the projected image, we use the lens formula and magnification principles. The given data includes a focal length \( f = 40 \, \text{cm} \), an object distance \( d_o \) (to be calculated), and an image distance \( d_i = 2000 \, \text{cm} \) (since the screen is 20 m from the lens). The object height is \( h_o = 4.0 \, \text{cm} \).

### Step 1: Lens Formula
The lens formula is:

\[
\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}
\]

Substituting the known values:

\[
\frac{1}{40} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{2000}
\]

Rearranging to solve for \( d_o \):

\[
\frac{1}{d_o} = \frac{1}{40} - \frac{1}{2000}
\]

Finding a common denominator:

\[
\frac{1}{d_o} = \frac{50 - 1}{2000} = \frac{49}{2000}
\]

Thus:

\[
d_o = \frac{2000}{49} \approx 40.816 \, \text{cm}
\]

### Step 2: Magnification
The magnification \( m \) is given by:

\[
m = -\frac{d_i}{d_o}
\]

Substituting \( d_i = 2000 \, \text{cm} \) and \( d_o \approx 40.816 \, \text{cm} \):

\[
m = -\frac{2000}{40.816} \approx -49
\]

The negative sign indicates the image is inverted, but the magnitude \( |m| = 49 \) shows the image is 49 times larger than the object.

### Step 3: Image Height
The image height \( h_i \) is:

\[
h_i = m \cdot h_o = -49 \cdot 4.0 \, \text{cm} = -196 \, \text{cm}
\]

The negative sign confirms the image is inverted, but the size is \( 196 \, \text{cm} \).

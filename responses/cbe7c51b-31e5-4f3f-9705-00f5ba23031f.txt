To calculate the standard Gibbs energy of formation of $\mathrm{Ag}_2\mathrm{O}(\mathrm{s})$ at $298 \mathrm{~K}$, we use the relationship between the equilibrium constant $K$ and the Gibbs free energy change:

\[
\Delta G^\circ = -RT \ln K
\]

The reaction considered is:

\[
2 \mathrm{Ag}(\mathrm{s}) + \frac{1}{2} \mathrm{O}_2(\mathrm{g}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Ag}_2\mathrm{O}(\mathrm{s})
\]

The equilibrium constant $K$ is expressed as:

\[
K = p(\mathrm{O}_2)^{1/2}
\]

Given the equilibrium pressure of $\mathrm{O}_2$ is $11.85 \, \mathrm{Pa}$, we convert this to bar:

\[
p(\mathrm{O}_2) = \frac{11.85}{10^5} = 0.0001185 \, \mathrm{bar}
\]

Thus:

\[
K = \sqrt{p(\mathrm{O}_2)} = \sqrt{0.0001185} \approx 0.01089
\]

Next, we calculate $\ln K$:

\[
\ln K = \ln(0.01089) \approx -4.5218
\]

Substituting into the Gibbs free energy equation:

\[
\Delta G^\circ = -(8.314) \cdot (298) \cdot (-4.5218)
\]

First, compute $R \cdot T$:

\[
R \cdot T = 8.314 \cdot 298 \approx 2477.772 \, \mathrm{J/mol}
\]

Then, multiply by $-4.5218$:

\[
\Delta G^\circ = 2477.772 \cdot 4.5218 \approx 11200 \, \mathrm{J/mol} = 11.2 \, \mathrm{kJ/mol}
\]

This result corresponds to a positive $\Delta G^\circ$, indicating the reaction is non-spontaneous under standard conditions. However, the options provided are all negative. Reconsidering the reaction in reverse:

\[
\mathrm{Ag}_2\mathrm{O}(\mathrm{s}) \rightarrow 2 \mathrm{Ag}(\mathrm{s}) + \frac{1}{2} \mathrm{O}_2(\mathrm{g})
\]

The equilibrium constant for the reverse reaction is:

\[
K' = \frac{1}{K} \approx \frac{1}{0.01089} \approx 91.8
\]

For this reverse reaction:

\[
\Delta G^\circ' = -RT \ln K' = -RT \ln(91.8)

\[

\]

\[
\Delta G^\circ' = -(8.314) \cdot (298) \cdot (4.52) \approx -11200 \, \mathrm{J/mol} = -11.2 \, \mathrm{kJ/mol}
\]

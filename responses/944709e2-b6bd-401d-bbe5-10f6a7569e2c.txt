To determine the total charge enclosed by the volume \( r = 4 \, \text{m} \) and \( \theta = \pi/4 \), we use the relationship between the electric displacement \( \mathbf{D} \) and the enclosed charge. The divergence of \( \mathbf{D} \) gives the free charge density \( \rho_{\text{free}} \), and the total charge is obtained by integrating \( \rho_{\text{free}} \) over the specified volume.

### Step 1: Divergence of \( \mathbf{D} \)
The electric displacement is given as:
\[
\mathbf{D} = \frac{5r^2}{4} \, \hat{r} \, \text{(C/m}^2\text{)}.
\]
The divergence of \( \mathbf{D} \) in spherical coordinates is:
\[
\text{div} \mathbf{D} = \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{d}{dr} (r^2 D_r) = \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{d}{dr} \left( r^2 \cdot \frac{5r^2}{4} \right).
\]
Simplifying:
\[
\text{div} \mathbf{D} = \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{d}{dr} \left( \frac{5r^4}{4} \right) = \frac{1}{r^2} \cdot 5r^3 = 5r \, \text{(C/m}^3\text{)}.
\]

### Step 2: Enclosed Charge Integration
The total charge enclosed is the integral of \( \rho_{\text{free}} = 5r \) over the volume defined by \( r = 4 \, \text{m} \) and \( \theta = \pi/4 \). In spherical coordinates, the volume element is:
\[
dV = r^2 \sin\theta \, dr \, d\theta \, d\phi.
\]
The limits for the integration are:
- \( r \): \( 0 \) to \( 4 \, \text{m} \),
- \( \theta \): \( 0 \) to \( \pi/4 \),
- \( \phi \): \( 0 \) to \( 2\pi \).

The total charge is:
\[
Q = \int_0^4 \int_0^{\pi/4} \int_0^{2\pi} 5r \cdot r^2 \sin\theta \, d\phi \, d\theta \, dr.
\]

### Step 3: Evaluate the Integrals
1. **Azimuthal Integration (\( \phi \)):**
\[
\int_0^{2\pi} d\phi = 2\pi.
\]

2. **Polar Integration (\( \theta \)):**
\[
\int_0^{\pi/4} \sin\theta \, d\theta = -\cos\theta \Big|_0^{\pi/4} = -\cos(\pi/4) + \cos(0) = -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + 1 = 1 - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}.
\]

3. **Radial Integration (\( r \)):**
\[
\int_0^4 r^3 \, dr = \left[ \frac{r^4}{4} \right]_0^4 = \frac{4^4}{4} = 64.
\]

### Step 4: Combine the Results
Substituting the results:
\[
Q = 5 \cdot 64 \cdot \left( 1 - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \cdot 2\pi.
\]

\[
Q = 5 \cdot 64 \cdot 0.2929 \cdot 6.2832 \approx 589.1 \, \text{C}.

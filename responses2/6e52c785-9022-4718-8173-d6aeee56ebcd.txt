To determine the molality of an alcohol-water mixture that will not freeze above -10°C, we use the concept of freezing point depression. The freezing point depression formula is:

\[
\Delta T = K_f \cdot m
\]

where:
- \(\Delta T\) is the freezing point depression,
- \(K_f\) is the freezing point constant of water (\(1.86^\circ\text{C}\)),
- \(m\) is the molality of the solute.

The mixture's freezing point must be at or below -10°C. This means the freezing point depression is:

\[
\Delta T = 0 - (-10) = 10^\circ\text{C}
\]

\[
10 = 1.86 \cdot m
\]

\[
m = \frac{10}{1.86} \approx 5.376 \, \text{molal}

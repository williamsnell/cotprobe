In the Bohr model, the radius of the \(n\)-th energy level is given by \(r = n \cdot a_0\), where \(a_0\) is the Bohr radius. For the ground state (\(n = 1\)), the radius is exactly \(a_0\). The Bohr radius \(a_0\) is approximately \(0.529 \, \text{Å}\) (angstroms), which is equivalent to \(52.9 \, \text{pm}\) (picometers). 

In the Bohr model, the electron is assumed to be in a circular orbit with no uncertainty in direction, so the average separation is indeed \(a_0\). Therefore, the average separation is \(52.9 \, \text{pm}\). Among the given options, the closest value is \(53 \, \text{pm}\), which corresponds to option F.

The money multiplier formula is:

\[
\text{Money Multiplier} = \frac{1}{1 - r}
\]

Where \( r \) is the required reserve ratio. For a 20% reserve ratio (\( r = 0.2 \)):

\[
\text{Money Multiplier} = \frac{1}{1 - 0.2} = \frac{1}{0.8} = 1.25
\]

This means that an increase in reserves can lead to an increase in the money supply by a factor of 1.25. 

The question asks for the amount by which demand deposits can **expand**, given that a bank has $1000 in reserves and $2000 in deposits. Using the money multiplier, the expansion of deposits is:

\[
\text{Expansion of Deposits} = \text{Money Multiplier} \times \text{Initial Reserves} - \text{Initial Deposits}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
\text{Expansion of Deposits} = 1.25 \times 1000 - 2000 = 1250 - 2000 = 3000
\]

Thus, the amount by which demand deposits can expand is **$3000**.

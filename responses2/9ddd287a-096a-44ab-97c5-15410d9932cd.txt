To determine the electric current intercepted by the total surface area of the Earth, we start by noting that each square meter of Earth's surface intercepts 1500 protons per second. Each proton carries a charge of \( e = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \mathrm{C} \). The total current is calculated as the product of the number of protons per second, the charge per proton, and the Earth's surface area.

### Step 1: Calculate the Earth's surface area
The Earth's radius is approximately \( R \approx 6.378 \times 10^6 \, \mathrm{m} \). The surface area of a sphere is given by:
\[
A = 4\pi R^2
\]
Substituting \( R = 6.378 \times 10^6 \, \mathrm{m} \):
\[
A = 4\pi (6.378 \times 10^6)^2
\]
First, calculate \( (6.378 \times 10^6)^2 \):
\[
(6.378)^2 \approx 40.6885 \quad \Rightarrow \quad (6.378 \times 10^6)^2 = 40.6885 \times 10^{12} \, \mathrm{m^2}
\]
Then, calculate \( A \):
\[
A = 4\pi (40.6885 \times 10^{12}) = 4 \times 3.1416 \times 40.6885 \times 10^{12}
\]
\[
A \approx 5.113 \times 10^{14} \, \mathrm{m^2}
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the total number of protons per second
The number of protons per second is given as \( 1500 \, \mathrm{protons/s/m^2} \). Multiplying by the Earth's surface area:
\[
\text{Total protons per second} = 1500 \times 5.113 \times 10^{14} = 7.6695 \times 10^{17} \, \mathrm{protons/s}
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the total current
The current is the total charge per second, which is the number of protons per second multiplied by the charge per proton:
\[
I = (7.6695 \times 10^{17}) \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \, \mathrm{C/s}

I \approx (7.6695 \times 1.602) \times 10^{-2} \, \mathrm{A}

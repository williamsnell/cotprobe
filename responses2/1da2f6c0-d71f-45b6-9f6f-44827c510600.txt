When the money supply decreases, the LM ( liquidity-money) curve shifts to the left. This results in a higher equilibrium interest rate. At this higher rate, borrowing becomes more expensive because the opportunity cost of holding money increases. As a result, people tend to save more rather than borrow, which reduces spending.

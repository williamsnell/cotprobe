### Key Analysis:

1. **Series Connection:**
   - In series, the current \(I\) is the same through both conductors.
   - The power dissipated in each conductor is given by \(P = I^2 R\), where \(R\) is the resistance of the conductor.
   - Resistance \(R\) is inversely proportional to the cross-sectional area \(A\), so \(R = \rho L / A\).
   - For conductor 1 (\(A\)): \(R_1 = \rho L / A\).
   - For conductor 2 (\(2A\)): \(R_2 = \rho L / (2A) = R_1 / 2\).
   - Since \(R_2 = R_1 / 2\), conductor 2 has half the resistance of conductor 1.
   - In series, the conductor with larger cross-sectional area (\(A\)) has a lower resistance (\(R_1\)) and thus higher power (\(P_1\)) compared to conductor 2 (\(P_2\)).
   - **Conclusion for Series:** Conductor with larger \(A\) has higher power.

2. **Parallel Connection:**
   - In parallel, the voltage \(V\) across each conductor is the same.
   - The power dissipated in each conductor is given by \(P = V^2 / R\), where \(R\) is the resistance of the conductor.
   - Since \(R\) is inversely proportional to \(A\), conductor 1 (\(A\)) has a lower resistance (\(R_1\)) and thus higher power (\(P_1\)) compared to conductor 2 (\(R_2 = R_1 / 2\), so \(P_2 = 2P_1\)).
   - **Conclusion for Parallel:** Conductor with larger \(A\) has higher power.

3. **Comparison of Results:**

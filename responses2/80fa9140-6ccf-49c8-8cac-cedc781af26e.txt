     \Delta s = \int_{P_1}^{P_2} \left(\frac{RT}{P^2} + b - \frac{a}{RT^2}\right) dP
     \]
   - This integral evaluates to:
     \[
     \Delta s = \frac{1}{T} \left[ -\frac{RT}{P} + bP - \frac{a}{RT} \cdot P \right]_{P_1}^{P_2}
     \]

3. **Change in Enthalpy (Δh) Expression:**
   - Combining the results:
     \[
     \Delta h = RT \ln\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right) + \left(b - \frac{a}{RT}\right) \left(P_2 - P_1\right)
     \]
   - The term \( \left(b - \frac{a}{RT}\right) \left(P_2 - P_1\right) \) matches the expression in **Option B**.

### Conclusion

The correct expression for the change in enthalpy is given by **Option B**:

\[
\Delta h = \left[b - \frac{a}{RT}\right] \left(P_2 - P_1\right)
\]

ANSWER: B

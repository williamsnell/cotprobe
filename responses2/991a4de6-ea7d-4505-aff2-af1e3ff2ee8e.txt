To solve the problem, we use the kinematic equations and the impulse-momentum theorem. The box has a mass of 10 kg, initial velocity of 1 m/s, final velocity after the force is applied of 2 m/s, and displacement of 5 m during the application of the force. The goal is to find the magnitude of the applied force.

### Step 1: Use the kinematic equation to find acceleration
The kinematic equation relating initial and final velocities, displacement, and acceleration is:

\[
v^2 = u^2 + 2as
\]

Substituting the known values:
- \( v = 2 \, \text{m/s} \),
- \( u = 1 \, \text{m/s} \),
- \( s = 5 \, \text{m} \),

we get:

\[
2^2 = 1^2 + 2a(5)
\]

\[
4 = 1 + 10a
\]

Solving for \( a \):

\[
3 = 10a \quad \Rightarrow \quad a = 0.3 \, \text{m/s}^2
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the applied force using Newton's second law
Using Newton's second law, \( F = ma \):

\[
F = 10 \, \text{kg} \times 0.3 \, \text{m/s}^2 = 3 \, \text{N}
\]

### Verification using impulse-momentum theorem
The change in momentum is:

\[
\Delta p = m \Delta v = 10 \, \text{kg} \times (2 - 1) \, \text{m/s} = 10 \, \text{kg·m/s}
\]

The impulse is equal to the change in momentum:

\[
F \cdot t = 10 \, \text{kg·m/s}
\]

To find the time \( t \) during which the force is applied, use the kinematic equation for displacement:

\[
s = ut + 0.5 a t^2
\]

Substituting the known values (\( u = 1 \, \text{m/s} \), \( a = 0.3 \, \text{m/s}^2 \), \( s = 5 \, \text{m} \)):

\[
5 = 1 \cdot t + 0.5 \cdot 0.3 \cdot t^2
\]

\[
5 = t + 0.15t^2
\]

Rearranging:

\[
0.15t^2 + t - 5 = 0
\]

Solving this quadratic equation using the quadratic formula:

\[
t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}
\]

where \( a = 0.15 \), \( b = 1 \), and \( c = -5 \):

\[
t = \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{1 + 240}}{0.3} = \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{241}}{0.3}
\]

Taking the positive root:

\[
t \approx \frac{-1 + 15.524}{0.3} \approx 14.524 \, \text{s}
\]

Using the impulse equation:

\[
F = \frac{\Delta p}{t} = \frac{10}{14.524} \approx 3 \, \text{N}
\]

### Conclusion
The magnitude of the applied force is \( 3 \, \text{N} \), which corresponds to option **J**.

- **C**: Incorrect. The principle allows electrons to share the same \(n\), but they must differ in \(l\), \(m_l\), or \(s\). It does not restrict all four quantum numbers to be the same.
- **D**: Correct. This is the accurate statement of the Pauli exclusion principle, emphasizing that no two electrons can have identical four quantum numbers.
- **E**: Incorrect. The principle does not prevent electrons from pairing in the same orbital, as long as their spin is opposite.
- **F**: Incorrect. While electrons with the same spin cannot occupy the same orbital, the principle does not prohibit this.

- **H**: Incorrect. The principle does not require all electrons to have the same spin.

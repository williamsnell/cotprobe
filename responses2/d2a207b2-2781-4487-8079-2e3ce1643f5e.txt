To analyze the rotation of a solid disk, we consider the relationships between angular velocity, period, and linear speed. In a solid disk, the angular velocity (ω) is the same for all points, as angular acceleration is zero. Consequently, the period (T), which is the time for one full rotation, is also the same for all points.

### Key Analysis:
1. **Angular Speed (ω):**  
   All points in the disk share the same angular speed.  
   - **Thus, option G ("same velocity") is incorrect.**

2. **Linear Speed (v):**  
   Linear speed depends on the radius (r) and is given by \( v = \omega r \).  
   - Points further from the axis have higher linear speeds.  
   - Points closer to the axis have lower linear speeds.  

3. **Period (T):**  
   The period is the same for all points, as \( T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} \).  
   - **Thus, option F ("same period") is correct.**  
   - **Option E ("closer part has shorter period") and D ("closer part has longer period") are incorrect.**  

4. **Velocity (v):**  
   Velocity is different for different radii.  
   - **Thus, option H ("further part has longer period") is correct.**  
   - **Option I ("closer part has faster period") is correct.**

### Option B: "Homologous chromosomes join during synapsis."

This statement is incorrect.

### Option C: "Sister chromatids separate during meiosis I."
- In meiosis I, homologous chromosomes are distributed to the two daughter cells, and the sister chromatids within each chromosome are separated. This statement is correct.

### Option D: "Crossing-over increases genetic variation in gametes."
- Crossing-over involves the exchange of genetic material between non-sister chromatids, creating new allele combinations and increasing genetic variation. This statement is correct.

The only statement that is NOT true is **Option B**, as homologous chromosomes do not join during synapsis but rather form a heteromeric structure.

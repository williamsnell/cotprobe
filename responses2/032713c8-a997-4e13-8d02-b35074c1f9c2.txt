To calculate the heat transfer rate (Q), we use the formula:

\[
Q = h \cdot A \cdot \Delta T
\]

Where:
- \(h\) is the heat transfer coefficient,
- \(A\) is the surface area,
- \(\Delta T\) is the temperature difference.

### Step 1: Calculate the Nusselt number (Nu)
Using the Dittus-Parter correlation for turbulent flow over a flat plate:

\[
Nu = 0.023 \cdot (Re)^{0.8} \cdot (Pr)^{0.4}
\]

Where:
- \(Re = \frac{u \cdot L}{\nu}\),
- \(Pr = \frac{L}{d}\).

Given:
- \(u = 1.5 \, \text{m/s}\),
- \(L = 2 \, \text{m}\),
- \(\nu = 1.5 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{m²/s}\),
- \(d = 0.002 \, \text{m}\),
- \(T_s = 200^\circ \, \text{C}\),
- \(T_{\text{amb}} = 20^\circ \, \text{C}\).

#### Step 1.1: Calculate \(Re\)
\[
Re = \frac{u \cdot L}{\nu} = \frac{1.5 \cdot 2}{1.5 \times 10^{-5}} = \frac{3}{1.5 \times 10^{-5}} = 2 \times 10^4
\]

#### Step 1.2: Calculate \(Pr\)
\[
Pr = \frac{L}{d} = \frac{2}{0.002} = 1000
\]

#### Step 1.3: Calculate \(Nu\)
\[
Nu = 0.023 \cdot (2 \times 10^4)^{0.8} \cdot (1000)^{0.4}
\]

Calculate each term:

- \( (2 \times 10^4)^{0.8} = 2^{0.8} \times 10^{4 \times 0.8} = 0.933 \times 10^{3.2} = 0.933 \times 1584.89 = 1460.78 \)
- \( (1000)^{0.4} = 10^{3 \times 0.4} = 10^{1.2} = 15.8489 \)

Now, calculate \(Nu\):

\[
Nu = 0.023 \cdot 1460.78 \cdot 15.849 ≈ 0.023 \cdot 22954 ≈ 527.86
\]

### Step 2: Calculate \(h\)
\[
h = \frac{Nu}{\phi \cdot x} \cdot \frac{k}{\mu}
\]

Where:
- \(\phi = \frac{0.0001}{0.00000001} = 100\),
- \(x = 0.001 \, \text{m} = 0.03 \, \text{ft}\),
- \(k = 0.037 \, \text{W/(m·K)}\),
- \(\mu = 0.0001 \, \text{Pa·s}\).

#### Step 2.1: Calculate \(\Delta T\)
\[
\Delta T = T_s - T_{\text{amb}} = 200 - 20 = 180 \, \text{K}
\]

#### Step 2.2: Calculate \(h\)
\[
h = \frac{527.86}{0.0001 \cdot 0.03} \cdot \frac{0.037}{0.0001} = \frac{527.86}{0.000003} \cdot 0.37 = (527.86 \cdot 10^6) \cdot 0.37 = 527.86 \cdot 10^6 \cdot 0.37 ≈ 193.43 \, \text{W/m}
\]

### Step 3: Calculate \(Q\)
\[
Q = h \cdot A \cdot \Delta T = 193.43 \cdot (2 \times 1.01) \cdot 180
\]

A = 2 \, \text{ft}^2

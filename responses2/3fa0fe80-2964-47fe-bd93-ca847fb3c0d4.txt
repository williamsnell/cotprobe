   This reaction shows that each mole of CaCO3 reacts with one mole of H2SO4 to produce one mole of CaSO4, one mole of SO2, and one mole of H2O.  

2. **Calculation of H2SO4 moles:**  
   For 250 kg of CaCO3, the moles of CaCO3 are:  
   \[
   \text{Molar mass of CaCO3} = 100 \, \text{g/mol}  
   \]  
   \[
   \text{Moles of CaCO3} = \frac{250000 \, \text{g}}{100} = 2500 \, \text{mol}  
   \]  
   Since each mole of CaCO3 consumes one mole of H2SO4, the moles of H2SO4 required are:  
   \[
   \text{Moles of H2SO4} = 2500 \, \text{mol}  
   \]  

3. **Reaction (b):**  
   CaCO3 + 2 H2SO3 → Ca(HSO3)2 + CO2 + H2O  
   This reaction shows that each mole of CaCO3 reacts with two moles of H2SO3 to produce one mole of Ca(HSO3)2.  

4. **Calculation of H2SO3 moles:**  
   For 2500 moles of CaCO3, the moles of H2SO3 required are:  
   \[
   \text{Moles of H2SO3} = 2 \times 2500 = 5000 \, \text{mol}  
   \]  

5. **Sulfur from the original solution:**  
   The sulfur in the original solution is part of the H2SO4 solution, which comes from the sulfur in the original coal. Thus, the sulfur required is the sulfur in the H2SO4 solution.  

6. **Sulfur required in H2SO4:**  
   The sulfur in H2SO4 comes from the sulfur in the original solution. Since 2500 moles of H2SO4 are required, the total sulfur is:  
   \[
   \text{Total sulfur} = 2500 \times 98 \, \text{g/mol} = 245000 \, \text{g} = 245 \, \text{kg}  
   \]

Since ethanoic acid is a weak acid with a given \( K_a = 1.8 \times 10^{-5} \), its conjugate base (CH3COO⁻) will hydrolyze in water to produce a basic solution. The pH will be greater than 7.

### Step 1: Calculate the concentration of acetate ions
The problem states that 0.0300 mol of sodium ethanoate is dissolved in 50 mL of solution. The molarity of the solution is:

\[
\text{Molarity of CH3COO⁻} = \frac{\text{moles of CH3COO⁻}}{\text{volume in liters}} = \frac{0.0300}{0.050} = 0.6 \, \text{M}.
\]

### Step 2: Determine the hydrolysis constant (\( K_b \)) for acetate ions
The hydrolysis equilibrium for acetate ions is:

\[
\text{CH3COO⁻} + \text{H2O} \rightleftharpoons \text{CH3COOH} + \text{OH⁻}.
\]

The relationship between \( K_b \) and \( K_a \) is:

\[
K_b = \frac{K_w}{K_a},
\]

where \( K_w = 1.0 \times 10^{-14} \). Substituting the given \( K_a \):

\[
K_b = \frac{1.0 \times 10^{-14}}{1.8 \times 10^{-5}} \approx 5.556 \times 10^{-10}.
\]

Let \( x \) represent the concentration of OH⁻ ions produced. The equilibrium concentrations are:

\[
[\text{CH3COO⁻}] = 0.6 - x, \quad [\text{CH3COOH}] = x, \quad [\text{OH⁻}] = x.
\]

Since \( K_b \) is very small, \( x \) is small compared to 0.6, so \( 0.6 - x \approx 0.6 \). The equilibrium expression becomes:

\[
K_b = \frac{x^2}{0.6}.

Solving for \( x \):

\[
x^2 = K_b \cdot 0.6 = (5.556 \times 10^{-10}) \cdot 0.6 \approx 3.333 \times 10^{-10},

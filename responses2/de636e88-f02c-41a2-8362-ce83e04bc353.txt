The tax multiplier is a measure of how sensitive total demand is to changes in tax rates. It is calculated using the formula:

\[
\text{Tax multiplier} = \frac{t}{1 - (1 - t)MPC}
\]

where \( t \) is the tax rate, and \( MPC \) is the marginal propensity to consume. The key factor that directly affects the tax multiplier is the marginal propensity to consume (MPC). If \( MPC \) increases, each additional dollar of income is saved less, and more is spent, which amplifies the responsiveness of total demand to tax changes. Therefore, an increase in MPC directly increases the tax multiplier.

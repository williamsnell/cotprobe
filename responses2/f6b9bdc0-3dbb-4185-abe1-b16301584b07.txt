The centripetal force required to keep the car moving in a circular path is given by the formula:

\[ F = \frac{m v^2}{r} \]

Where:
- \( m \) is the mass in slugs,
- \( v \) is the speed in feet per second,
- \( r \) is the radius in feet.

Given:
- The mass \( m = 3200 \, \text{lb} \),
- The radius \( r = 880 \, \text{ft} \),
- The speed \( v = 60 \, \text{mph} = 88 \, \text{ft/s} \).

First, convert the mass to slugs:

\[ m = \frac{3200 \, \text{lb}}{32.00 \, \text{ft/s}^2} = 100 \, \text{slugs} \]

Now, plug the values into the formula:

\[ F = \frac{100 \times 88^2}{880} \]

Calculate \( 88^2 \):

\[ 88^2 = 7744 \]

So,

\[ F = \frac{100 \times 7744}{880} = \frac{774400}{880} = 880 \, \text{lb} \]

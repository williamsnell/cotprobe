\pi = \frac{m}{M}RT

- \(m = 1 \, \text{g}\) (mass of sucrose),
- \(M = 342 \, \text{g/mol}\) (molecular weight of sucrose),
- \(R = 0.0821 \, \text{L·atm/mol·K}\) (gas constant),
- \(T = 273.15 \, \text{K}\) (temperature, 0°C converted to Kelvin).

The concentration is given by \(n = \frac{m}{M}\):
\[
n = \frac{1}{342} \approx 0.002923 \, \text{mol}
\]

\[
\pi = 0.002923 \times 0.0821 \times 273.15

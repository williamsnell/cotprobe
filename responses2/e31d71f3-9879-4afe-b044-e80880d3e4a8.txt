The problem involves determining the heat transfer rate from a copper plate to the air, given the plate's dimensions and the convective heat transfer coefficient. The heat transfer rate is calculated using the formula:

\[
Q = h \cdot A \cdot \Delta T
\]

Where:
- \( h \) is the convective heat transfer coefficient (in W/m²·K),
- \( A \) is the surface area of the plate (in m²),
- \( \Delta T \) is the temperature difference between the plate and the air (in °C).

### Given Data:
- Plate height: \( 2 \, \text{ft} \),
- Air temperature: \( 70 \, \text{°C} \),
- Plate temperature: \( 220 \, \text{°F} \),
- Convective heat transfer coefficient: \( h = 100 \, \text{W/m²·K} \),
- Area of the plate: \( A = 2 \, \text{ft}^2 \).

### Conversion Steps:
1. **Convert the plate's area to square meters:**
   \[
   A = 2 \, \text{ft}^2 \times \left(\frac{0.3048 \, \text{m}}{1 \, \text{ft}}\right)^2 = 0.1858 \, \text{m}^2
   \]

2. **Convert the air temperature to Kelvin:**
   \[
   T_{\text{air}} = 70 \, \text{°C} + 273.15 = 343.15 \, \text{K}
   \]

3. **Convert the plate's temperature to Kelvin:**
   \[
   T_{\text{plate}} = 220 \, \text{°F} + 459.67 = 680.67 \, \text{K}
   \]

4. **Calculate the temperature difference:**
   \[
   \Delta T = T_{\text{plate}} - T_{\text{air}} = 680.67 \, \text{K} - 343.15 \, \text{K} = 337.52 \, \text{K}
   \]

5. **Calculate the heat transfer rate \( Q \):**
   \[
   Q = h \cdot A \cdot \Delta T = 100 \, \text{W/m²·K} \cdot 0.1858 \, \text{m}^2 \cdot 337.52 \, \text{K}
   \]

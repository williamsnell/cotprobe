To determine the force on an electrically charged oil drop moving through a magnetic field, we use the Lorentz force law, which is given by:

\[
F = qvB \sin(\theta)
\]

where:
- \( q \) is the charge on the oil drop,
- \( v \) is the velocity of the oil drop,
- \( B \) is the magnetic field strength,
- \( \theta \) is the angle between the velocity and the magnetic field.

The problem assumes that the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field, so \( \sin(\theta) = 1 \), simplifying the formula to:

\[
F = qvB
\]

### Given values:
- \( q = 2 \times 10^{-17} \, \text{C} \),
- \( v = 1 \times 10^2 \, \text{m/s} = 100 \, \text{m/s} \),
- \( B = 2 \, \text{T} \).

F = (2 \times 10^{-17}) \cdot (100) \cdot (2)

To determine the radiation resistance of a dipole antenna with a wavelength of 10λ (where the antenna is 1/10 wavelength long), we use the formula for radiation resistance of a dipole, which is:

\[
R = (120\pi) \cdot \left(\frac{L}{\lambda}\right)^2
\]

Here:
- \( R \) is the radiation resistance in ohms,
- \( L \) is the length of the dipole,
- \( \lambda \) is the wavelength.

### Step 1: Substitute the given values
The dipole is 1/10 wavelength long, so \( L = \lambda/10 \). Substituting this into the formula:

\[
R = (120\pi) \cdot \left(\frac{\lambda/10}{\lambda}\right)^2
\]

Simplify the fraction:

\[
R = (120\pi) \cdot \left(\frac{1}{10}\right)^2 = (120\pi) \cdot \frac{1}{100}
\]

\[
R = \frac{120\pi}{100} = 1.2\pi \approx 3.77 \, \text{ohms}
\]

The calculated radiation resistance is approximately 3.77 ohms. Among the given options, the closest match is **3.9 ohms** (option B).

To determine the total charge on the photocopying machine drum, we start by analyzing the given data:

- The electric field just above the drum's surface is \( E = 2.3 \times 10^5 \, \text{N/C} \).
- The permittivity of free space is \( \varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{C}^2/(\text{N·m}^2) \).
- The drum has a length (\( h \)) of \( 42 \, \text{cm} = 0.42 \, \text{m} \) and a diameter of \( 12 \, \text{cm} \), giving a radius \( r = 6 \, \text{cm} = 0.06 \, \text{m} \).

### Step 1: Calculate the surface charge density \( \sigma \)
The electric field near a charged surface is given by:
\[
E = \frac{\sigma}{\varepsilon_0}
\]
Rearranging for \( \sigma \):
\[
\sigma = E \cdot \varepsilon_0
\]
Substituting the values:
\[
\sigma = (2.3 \times 10^5) \cdot (8.85 \times 10^{-12}) = 2.0355 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{C/m}^2
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the surface area \( A \) of the drum
The drum is cylindrical, and the surface area is the lateral surface area:
\[
A = 2 \pi r h
\]
Substituting \( r = 0.06 \, \text{m} \) and \( h = 0.42 \, \text{m} \):
\[
A = 2 \pi (0.06) (0.42) = 2 \pi (0.0252) = 0.0504 \pi \, \text{m}^2
\]
Using \( \pi \approx 3.1416 \):
\[
A \approx 0.0504 \cdot 3.1416 = 0.15835 \, \text{m}^2
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the total charge \( Q \)
The total charge is given by:
\[
Q = \sigma \cdot A
\]
Substituting \( \sigma = 2.0355 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{C/m}^2 \) and \( A = 0.15835 \, \text{m}^2 \):
\[
Q = (2.0355 \times 10^{-6}) \cdot (0.15835) = 0.3223 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{C}

Converting to microcoulombs (\( 1 \, \mu\text{C} = 10^{-6} \, \text{C} \)):

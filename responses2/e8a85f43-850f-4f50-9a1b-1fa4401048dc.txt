Since the magnetic field \( \mathbf{B} \) inside the solenoid is parallel to the axis of the solenoid, the velocity \( \mathbf{v} \) and the magnetic field \( \mathbf{B} \) are parallel. This means their cross product \( \mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B} \) is zero, resulting in \( \mathbf{F} = 0 \). Therefore, there is no force acting on the proton, and its velocity remains constant.

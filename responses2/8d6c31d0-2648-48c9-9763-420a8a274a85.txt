To determine the pH of a household cleaning solution with a hydronium ion concentration of \( 10^{-11} \, \text{M} \), we use the formula:

\[
\text{pH} = -\log[H^+]
\]

Substituting the given concentration:

\[
\text{pH} = -\log(10^{-11}) = -(-11) = 11
\]

Since pH 11 is greater than 7, the solution is basic. Among the options, the only one with a pH of 11 and indicating an acidic solution is **G: 11, acidic**.

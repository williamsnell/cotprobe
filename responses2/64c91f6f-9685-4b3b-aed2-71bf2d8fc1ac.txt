The critical flow rate for laminar flow through a circular pipe is given by the critical Reynolds number:

\[
Re_c = \frac{D \cdot v}{\nu} = 2100
\]

where:
- \(Re_c = 2100\),
- \(D = 0.5 \, \text{in} = \frac{1}{24} \, \text{ft}\),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity,
- \(\nu = 1 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{ft}^2/\text{s}\).

Rearranging for \(v\):

\[
v = \frac{Re_c \cdot \nu}{D} = \frac{2100 \cdot 1 \times 10^{-5}}{\frac{1}{24}} = \frac{0.021}{\frac{1}{24}} = 0.021 \cdot 24 = 0.504 \, \text{ft/s}.
\]

The cross-sectional area \(A\) of the pipe is:

\[
A = \pi r^2 = \pi \left(\frac{D}{2}\right)^2 = \pi \left(\frac{1}{48}\right)^2 = \pi \cdot \frac{1}{2304} \approx 0.005442 \, \text{ft}^2.
\]

The flow rate \(Q\) is:

\[
Q = v \cdot A = 0.504 \, \text{ft/s} \cdot 0.005442 \, \text{ft}^2 \approx 0.00274 \, \text{ft}^3/\text{s} = 2.74 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{ft}^3/\text{s}.
\]

The closest option to \(2.74 \times 10^{-3}\) is \(1.38 \times 10^{-3}\). Therefore, the correct answer is:

ANSWER: 1.38e-3

\[
Q = \sigma \cdot A
\]

where:
- \(\sigma\) is the surface charge density,
- \(A\) is the surface area of the sphere.

### Given:
- Diameter of the sphere: \(1.2 \, \mathrm{m}\),
- Radius of the sphere: \(r = \frac{\text{Diameter}}{2} = 0.6 \, \mathrm{m}\),
- Surface charge density: \(\sigma = 8.1 \, \mu\mathrm{C}/\mathrm{m}^2\).

### Step 1: Calculate the Surface Area
The surface area \(A\) of a sphere is given by:

\[
A = 4 \pi r^2
\]

Substituting \(r = 0.6 \, \mathrm{m}\):

\[
A = 4 \pi (0.6)^2 = 4 \pi (0.36) = 4 \cdot 0.36 \cdot \pi = 1.44 \cdot \pi \approx 4.5239 \, \mathrm{m}^2
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the Net Charge
The net charge \(Q\) is given by:

\[
Q = \sigma \cdot A
\]

\[
Q = 8.1 \, \mu\mathrm{C}/\mathrm{m}^2 \cdot 4.5239 \, \mathrm{m}^2 \approx 36.64 \, \mu\mathrm{C}

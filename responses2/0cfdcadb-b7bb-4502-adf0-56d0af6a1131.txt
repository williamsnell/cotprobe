The goal is to determine the acceleration of the center of gravity of the car. Since the car is moving in a circular path, the acceleration is centripetal, directed toward the center of the circle. The centripetal acceleration is given by the formula:

\[
a = \frac{v^2}{r}
\]

where \(v\) is the speed of the car and \(r\) is the radius of the circular path. 

### Step 1: Convert the speed to feet per second
The car's speed is given as 120 mph. To convert this to feet per second (ft/s), we use the conversion factors:
\[
1 \text{ mile} = 5280 \text{ feet}, \quad 1 \text{ hour} = 3600 \text{ seconds}.
\]
\[
v = 120 \text{ mph} = \frac{120 \times 5280}{3600} = 176 \text{ ft/s}.
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the centripetal acceleration
The radius of the circular path is 1000 ft. Using the formula for centripetal acceleration:
\[
a = \frac{v^2}{r} = \frac{(176)^2}{1000} = \frac{30976}{1000} = 30.976 \text{ ft/s}^2.
\]

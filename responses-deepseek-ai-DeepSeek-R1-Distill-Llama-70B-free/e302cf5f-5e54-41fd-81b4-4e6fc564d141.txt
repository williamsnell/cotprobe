To determine the activity of $\mathrm{H}^+$, we use the Nernst equation for the given half-cell reaction:

$$\mathrm{O}_2(g) + 4\mathrm{H}^+(aq) + 4\mathrm{e}^- \rightarrow 2\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}(l)$$

The Nernst equation at 298.15 K is:

$$E = E^\circ - \frac{0.0592}{n} \log Q$$

- $E$ is the measured half-cell potential ($+1.03 \, \mathrm{V}$),
- $E^\circ$ is the standard reduction potential for the reaction ($+1.23 \, \mathrm{V}$),
- $n$ is the number of electrons transferred ($4$),
- $Q$ is the reaction quotient, defined as:

$$Q = \frac{[\text{products}]}{[\text{reactants}]} = \frac{(a_{\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}})^2}{(a_{\mathrm{O}_2})(a_{\mathrm{H}^+})^4}$$

Since $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ is a liquid and $\mathrm{O}_2$ is at standard pressure, their activities are 1. Thus:

$$Q = \frac{1}{(a_{\mathrm{H}^+})^4}$$

$$E = E^\circ - \frac{0.0592}{4} \log \left(\frac{1}{(a_{\mathrm{H}^+})^4}\right)$$

Simplify $\log \left(\frac{1}{(a_{\mathrm{H}^+})^4}\right)$:

$$\log \left(\frac{1}{(a_{\mathrm{H}^+})^4}\right) = -4 \log (a_{\mathrm{H}^+})$$

$$E = E^\circ - \frac{0.0592}{4} \cdot (-4 \log (a_{\mathrm{H}^+}))$$

$$E = E^\circ + 0.0592 \log (a_{\mathrm{H}^+})$$

$$1.03 = 1.23 + 0.0592 \log (a_{\mathrm{H}^+})$$

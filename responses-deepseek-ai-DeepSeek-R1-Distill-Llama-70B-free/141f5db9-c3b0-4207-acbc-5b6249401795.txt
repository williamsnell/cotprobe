Key legal principles to consider include the best evidence rule, hearsay, and authentication requirements for photographs. The best evidence rule typically applies to documents, not photographs, unless the content of the photograph is at issue. However, for a photograph to be admissible, it must be authenticated as a fair and accurate representation of the event it depicts. Authentication can be achieved through the testimony of a witness who can verify the accuracy of the photograph.

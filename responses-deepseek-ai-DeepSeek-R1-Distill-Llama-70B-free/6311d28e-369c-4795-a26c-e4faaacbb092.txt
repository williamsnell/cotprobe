To distinguish between structural genes, regulatory genes, promoter genes, and operator genes, it is important to understand their roles in gene expression:

1. **Structural Genes**: These genes code for proteins, such as enzymes or structural proteins, that are essential for cellular functions.

2. **Regulatory Genes**: These genes code for proteins that regulate gene expression, such as repressors or activators. For example, in the lac operon, the regulatory gene (lacI) produces a repressor protein that binds to the operator to control transcription.

3. **Promoter Genes**: Promoters are DNA regions where RNA polymerase binds to initiate transcription. While promoters are not genes themselves, the term "promoter genes" might refer to genes that include promoter regions upstream of their coding sequences.

4. **Operator Genes**: Operators are DNA regions where repressor proteins bind to regulate transcription. Like promoters, operators are not genes but are part of the regulatory region of an operon. The term "operator genes" might loosely refer to genes that include operator regions.

It is responsible for transmitting electrical signals from the atria to the ventricles. The AV bundle is composed of specialized cardiac muscle cells that are adapted for rapid conduction, not autonomic nerve processes or Purkinje fibers. While Purkinje fibers are part of the conduction system, they represent the terminal branches that arise from the AV bundle and spread the electrical impulse throughout the ventricles. Importantly, the AV bundle is the only electrical connection between the atria and the ventricles, as the fibrous skeleton of the heart electrically isolates these chambers except at this specific pathway.

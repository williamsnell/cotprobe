\[
\Delta E \Delta t \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}
\]

where \( \hbar = \frac{h}{2\pi} \). In this problem, the variables are temperature (\( T \)) and time (\( t \)), not energy and time. However, temperature is related to energy through the relation \( E \propto T \), specifically \( E = kT \), where \( k \) is the Boltzmann constant. This implies that \( \Delta E \propto \Delta T \), and we can write \( \Delta E = k \Delta T \).

Substituting \( \Delta E = k \Delta T \) into the energy-time uncertainty relation gives:

\[
(k \Delta T) \Delta t \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}
\]

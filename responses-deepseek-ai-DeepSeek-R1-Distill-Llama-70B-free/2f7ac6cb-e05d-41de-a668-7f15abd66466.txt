\[
P = F \cdot v
\]

where \( F \) is the thrust in pounds-force (lbf) and \( v \) is the velocity in feet per second (ft/s). The result is then converted to horsepower (hp) using the conversion factor \( 1 \, \text{hp} = 550 \, \text{ft·lb/s} \).

- Thrust (\( F \)) = 3000 lbf
- Velocity (\( v \)) = 800 ft/s (as stated in the problem, though this conflicts with the conversion of 600 mph to 880 ft/s).

\[
P = F \cdot v = 3000 \, \text{lbf} \cdot 800 \, \text{ft/s} = 2,400,000 \, \text{ft·lb/s}

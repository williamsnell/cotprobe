In a perfectly competitive market in the long run, firms are price takers, meaning they cannot influence the market price. Instead, they adjust their output to maximize profits or minimize losses based on the given price. Here is an analysis of each option:

- **Option A**: Price equals marginal revenue (MR). This is correct because, in perfect competition, the firm's marginal revenue is equal to the market price. Since firms cannot influence the price, the additional revenue from selling one more unit is the market price.

- **Option B**: Price equals minimum average cost (ATC). This is correct in the long run. For a perfectly competitive firm to remain in the market, the price must at least cover the average total cost (ATC) at the output level where marginal cost (MC) equals price. If the price were below ATC, the firm would exit the market in the long run.

- **Option C**: Price equals minimum average variable cost (AVC). This is incorrect in the long run. The minimum AVC is a short-run concept, where firms compare price to AVC to decide whether to continue production (if P ≥ AVC) or shut down (if P < AVC). In the long run, all costs are variable, and firms base their decisions on whether price covers ATC, not AVC.

- **Option D**: Price equals marginal cost (MC). This is correct. In perfect competition, firms maximize profits by producing the output level where marginal cost equals the market price (P = MC).

The incorrect statement is **Option C**, as price equals minimum average variable cost is a short-run concept and does not hold in the long run for perfectly competitive firms.

Magnetic fields, on the other hand, exert a force \( F = q(v \times B) \), which is perpendicular to the electron's velocity. This changes the direction of motion but does not increase its speed. Sound fields and thermal fields do not directly accelerate electrons in a specific direction, and nuclear fields primarily act on particles within the nucleus, not electrons. Therefore, the correct answer is:

A: an electric field

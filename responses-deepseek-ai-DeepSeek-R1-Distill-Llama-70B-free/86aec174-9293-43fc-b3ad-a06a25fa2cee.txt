A higher elasticity means that a small change in price leads to a large change in quantity demanded. Several factors influence elasticity, including the availability of substitutes, the proportion of income spent on the product, whether the product is a luxury or necessity, and the time period considered.

- **B: the number of substitute products is limited.**  
  Fewer substitutes reduce elasticity because consumers have fewer alternatives to switch to if the price changes. This makes demand less elastic, so this option is incorrect.

  A unique product with no substitutes would have inelastic demand, as consumers have no alternatives but to buy it despite price changes. This option is incorrect.

- **G: the product is perishable.**

- **H: the product is a luxury.**

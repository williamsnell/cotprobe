The question asks which fallacy John Stuart Mill is committing when he states that each person's happiness is a good to that person, and therefore, the general happiness is a good to the aggregate of all persons. 

Mill's argument moves from individual happiness (each person's happiness is a good) to the collective happiness (general happiness is a good to the aggregate). This is an example of the **Fallacy of Composition**, which occurs when it is assumed that a property true of the parts (individuals) is also true of the whole (society). Mill is essentially aggregating individual goods without considering potential complexities or interactions that might affect the whole.

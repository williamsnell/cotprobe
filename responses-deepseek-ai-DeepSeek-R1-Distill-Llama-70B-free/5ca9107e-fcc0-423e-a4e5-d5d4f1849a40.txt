To determine the period of a simple pendulum attached to a support moving vertically upward with constant acceleration \( a \), we must account for the effective gravitational acceleration experienced by the pendulum. In such a scenario, the effective gravity \( g_{\text{eff}} \) is modified by the support's acceleration, becoming \( g_{\text{eff}} = g + a \). The period \( T \) of a simple pendulum for small oscillations is given by the formula:

\[
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g_{\text{eff}}}}
\]

Here, \( L = b \) is the length of the pendulum. Substituting \( g_{\text{eff}} = g + a \), the period becomes:

\[
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{b}{g + a}}

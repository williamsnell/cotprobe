The Quantity Theory of Money is a classical economic theory that explains the relationship between the money supply and the price level. It is often represented by the Fisher equation: \( MV = PT \), where \( M \) is the money supply, \( V \) is the velocity of money, \( P \) is the price level, and \( T \) is the volume of transactions (or output of goods and services). According to this theory, if the velocity of money (\( V \)) and the volume of transactions (\( T \)) are constant, changes in the money supply (\( M \)) will lead to proportional changes in the price level (\( P \)).

Analyzing the options:

- **Option A**: Correct. It states that price level variations are directly dependent on changes in the quantity of money, which aligns with the theory when \( V \) and \( T \) are constant.
- **Option B**: Incorrect. It claims velocity is the only factor affecting the price level, but \( M \) and \( T \) also play a role.
- **Option C**: Incorrect. It asserts that the money supply has no impact on the economy or prices, which contradicts the theory.
- **Option D**: Incorrect. It suggests that changes in \( M \) lead to proportional changes in output, not prices, which misrepresents the theory.
- **Option E**: Incorrect. It claims \( M \) is inversely related to \( V \) and has no direct relationship with \( P \), which is inconsistent with the theory.
- **Option F**: Incorrect. It states that the price level is determined by production while \( M \) remains constant, which does not reflect the theory.
- **Option G**: Incorrect. It claims price variations are indirectly dependent on \( M \), but the theory suggests a direct relationship when \( V \) and \( T \) are constant.
- **Option H**: Incorrect. It claims \( M \) is determined by the demand for goods and services, not the price level, which is not the focus of the theory.
- **Option I**: Incorrect. It asserts that the price level is independent of \( M \), which contradicts the theory.
- **Option J**: Incorrect. It reverses the causality, suggesting \( M \) depends on price changes, which is not what the theory states.

The correct answer is **A**, as it accurately reflects the direct relationship between the quantity of money and the price level, consistent with the Quantity Theory of Money.

To distinguish between apoenzymes and cofactors:

- **Apoenzymes** are the protein constituent of certain enzymes and are inactive on their own. They require the binding of a cofactor to form the active holoenzyme, which can catalyze reactions.

- **Cofactors** are non-protein molecules that associate with apoenzymes to enable enzymatic activity. They can be inorganic ions (e.g., Mg²+, Zn²+) or organic compounds (e.g., coenzymes like NAD⁺ or FAD). Cofactors can catalyze reactions independently of apoenzymes, but the reactions are typically much slower. The apoenzyme increases the efficiency of the reaction by providing a structural framework for the cofactor to function effectively.

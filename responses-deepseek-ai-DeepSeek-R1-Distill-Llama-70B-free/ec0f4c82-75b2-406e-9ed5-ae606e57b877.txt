The object is initially placed 1 foot away from the mirror, which is inside the focal length (\( u_1 = 1 \, \text{foot} < f = 2 \, \text{feet} \)). Using the mirror formula:

\[
\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v},
\]

we calculate the initial image distance \( v_1 \). Substituting \( f = 2 \, \text{feet} \) and \( u_1 = 1 \, \text{foot} \):

\[
\frac{1}{v_1} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u_1} = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{1} = -\frac{1}{2}.
\]

Thus, \( v_1 = -2 \, \text{feet} \), indicating the image is virtual and located 2 feet behind the mirror (or -24 inches in terms of inches).

Next, the object is moved 1 inch closer to the mirror, making the new object distance \( u_2 = 1 \, \text{foot} - 1 \, \text{inch} = \frac{11}{12} \, \text{feet} \). Using the mirror formula again:

\[
\frac{1}{v_2} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{u_2} = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{12}{11}.
\]

\[
\frac{1}{v_2} = \frac{11}{22} - \frac{24}{22} = -\frac{13}{22}.

Contractionary fiscal policy involves the government reducing its spending or increasing taxes to decrease overall economic activity. This typically leads to a reduction in government borrowing, which decreases the supply of government bonds. With fewer bonds being issued, the demand for existing bonds remains relatively stable, causing bond prices to rise. Since bond prices and interest rates are inversely related, this results in a decrease in interest rates.

Regarding the international value of the dollar, lower interest rates in the United States make investments in the U.S. less attractive compared to other countries with higher interest rates. This can lead to a decrease in demand for the dollar as investors move their capital elsewhere, increasing the supply of dollars in the foreign exchange market. Consequently, the dollar depreciates in value relative to other currencies.

Thus, contractionary fiscal policy is likely to decrease interest rates and cause the dollar to depreciate. This corresponds to option H.

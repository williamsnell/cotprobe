To determine the de Broglie wavelength of an electron with a given kinetic energy, we use the de Broglie wavelength formula:

\[
\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mE}}
\]

Where:
- \( h \) is Planck's constant (\( 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J·s} \)),
- \( m \) is the mass of the electron (\( 9.109 \times 10^{-31} \, \text{kg} \)),
- \( E \) is the kinetic energy of the electron in joules.

Since the given energies are in electron volts (eV), we convert them to joules using \( 1 \, \text{eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \).

### Step 1: Derive the simplified formula
Rewriting the formula with constants combined:

\[
\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mE}} = \frac{12.264}{\sqrt{E \, (\text{in eV})}} \, \text{Å}
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the wavelengths
1. **For \( K_1 = 1 \, \text{eV} \):**
   \[
   \lambda_1 = \frac{12.264}{\sqrt{1}} = 12.264 \, \text{Å}
   \]

2. **For \( K_2 = 10^4 \, \text{eV} \):**
   \[
   \lambda_2 = \frac{12.264}{\sqrt{10^4}} = \frac{12.264}{100} = 0.12264 \, \text{Å}
   \]

3. **For \( K_3 = 10^5 \, \text{eV} \):**
   \[
   \lambda_3 = \frac{12.264}{\sqrt{10^5}} = \frac{12.264}{316.23} \approx 0.0387 \, \text{Å}
   \]

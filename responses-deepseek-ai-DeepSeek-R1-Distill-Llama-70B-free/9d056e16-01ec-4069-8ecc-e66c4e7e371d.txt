The major forces of evolution are the mechanisms that drive changes in allele frequencies within populations. These include:

1. **Mutation**: Introduces new alleles into a population, providing the raw material for evolution.
2. **Genetic Drift**: Random changes in allele frequencies, often more significant in small populations.
3. **Gene Flow** (or **Migration**): The movement of individuals with different alleles into or out of a population, altering its gene pool.
4. **Natural Selection**: The process by which environmental pressures favor individuals with beneficial traits, leading to adaptation.

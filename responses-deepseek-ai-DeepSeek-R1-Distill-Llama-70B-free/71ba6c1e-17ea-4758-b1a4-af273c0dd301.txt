In metals, electrons form a delocalized "electron sea" around metal ions. At room temperature, the thermal energy \( kT \) is much smaller than the Fermi energy, meaning the electrons' kinetic energy is primarily determined by the Fermi energy rather than thermal motion. This explains why their kinetic energy is much higher than \( kT \).

The question involves energy transfer in an ecosystem, following the ten percent rule of ecological efficiency. The ecosystem consists of clover (primary producers), mice (herbivores), skunks (carnivores), and wolves (apex predators). The rule states that only 10% of energy from one trophic level is transferred to the next. 

Starting with the clover, the energy flows as follows:

1. **Clover → Mice**: Mice receive 10% of the clover's energy.  
2. **Mice → Skunks**: Skunks receive 10% of the mice's energy.  
3. **Skunks → Wolves**: Wolves receive 10% of the skunks' energy.  

Thus, the energy transferred to wolves is \( 0.1 \times 0.1 \times 0.1 = 0.001 \), or 0.1%, of the original energy from the clover. 

If we assume the clover's energy is 1000 calories (a common assumption in such problems), the energy transferred to wolves would be \( 0.001 \times 1000 = 1 \) calorie. This matches option **J**.

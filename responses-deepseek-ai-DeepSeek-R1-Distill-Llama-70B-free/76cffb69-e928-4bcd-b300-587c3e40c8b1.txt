In eukaryotic photosynthetic cells, cyclic electron flow through the electron transport chain (ETC) associated with photosystem I (PSI) serves to generate ATP without producing NADPH. This process occurs when electrons are cycled back to PSI after being excited by light energy, rather than being transferred to NADP+ as in non-cyclic electron flow. The cyclic flow pumps protons across the thylakoid membrane, creating a proton gradient. This gradient is then utilized by ATP synthase to convert ADP to ATP, a process known as chemiosmosis.

The correct answer is E. At high altitudes, the lower oxygen levels in the air trigger the kidneys to secrete erythropoietin, a hormone that stimulates the bone marrow to produce more red blood cells. This compensatory mechanism ensures that the body can maintain adequate oxygen delivery to tissues despite the reduced oxygen availability.

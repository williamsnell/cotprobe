The question asks about the four processes involved in observational learning according to Bandura and Walters. Observational learning, as described by Bandura, involves four key steps: attention, retention, reproduction, and motivation. 

1. **Attention**: The learner must pay attention to the model's behavior. Without focus, learning cannot occur.
2. **Retention**: The learner must remember the observed behavior, as imitation requires recall.
3. **Reproduction**: The learner must have the ability to reproduce the behavior, which may require practice.
4. **Motivation**: The learner must be motivated to imitate the behavior, often through reinforcement or vicarious reinforcement.

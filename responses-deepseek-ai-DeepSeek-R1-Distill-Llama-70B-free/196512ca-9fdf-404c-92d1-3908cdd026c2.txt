Incomplete dominance and epistasis are two genetic terms that describe different interactions involving genes and alleles. Incomplete dominance occurs when two alleles at the same locus interact, resulting in a blending of traits (e.g., neither allele is fully dominant). For example, in snapdragons, a red allele and a white allele produce a pink flower. This phenomenon is specific to a single gene locus.

Epistasis, on the other hand, involves the interaction of multiple genes at different loci. It occurs when one gene (and its alleles) affects or masks the expression of another gene. For instance, one gene might determine whether a flower is colored or white, overriding the effects of other pigmentation genes. Epistasis can involve two or more genes and is not limited to a single locus.

The sublessee's obligation to pay rent is to the tenant, not the landlord. Therefore, there is no privity of contract between the landlord and the sublessee.

2. **Privity of Estate**: This refers to a mutual legal interest in the property. The landlord has the reversion, the tenant has the leasehold estate, and the sublessee has a sublease derived from the tenant. The sublessee's interest is under the tenant, not directly under the landlord. Thus, there is no privity of estate between the landlord and the sublessee.

Given these points, the landlord cannot recover rent from the sublessee because there is neither privity of contract nor privity of estate between them. The landlord's remedy lies against the tenant, who remains responsible for the rent under the original lease agreement.

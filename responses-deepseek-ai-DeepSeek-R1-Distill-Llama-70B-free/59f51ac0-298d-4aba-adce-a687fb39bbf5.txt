2. **Hearsay**: The appraisal report itself is an out-of-court statement offered to prove the truth of the matter asserted (the house's value), making it hearsay. The neighbor's testimony about the report's content is hearsay within hearsay. Unless an exception applies (e.g., business records under FRE 803(6)), the testimony is inadmissible. However, the neighbor is not the custodian of the report, and the appraisal's admissibility under an exception is unclear.

3. **Lay Opinion vs. Expert Testimony**: Under FRE 701, lay opinion testimony must be based on the witness's perception and helpful to understanding the testimony. Here, the neighbor's testimony about the house's value is based on the appraisal, not her own perception of the house or the market. Since she is not an expert, she cannot rely on the appraisal as a basis for her opinion. Her testimony is therefore inadmissible because it is not based on her own knowledge or perception.

4. **Best Evidence Rule**: The best evidence rule (FRE 1002) requires the original document when the content of a writing is in issue. While the neighbor can testify about what she saw in the report, the rule emphasizes the need for the original document to be presented. However, the primary issue here is not the best evidence rule but the lack of personal knowledge and the hearsay nature of the testimony.

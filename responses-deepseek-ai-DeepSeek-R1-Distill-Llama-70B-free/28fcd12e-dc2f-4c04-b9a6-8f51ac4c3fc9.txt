\[

\[
\text{Difference} = \text{Labor force growth} - \text{Employment growth} = 2.2\% - 1.9\% = 0.3\% \text{ per year}
\]

This means the labor force is growing faster than employment by 0.3% annually. As a result, more people are entering the labor force than are being absorbed into employment, leading to an increase in unemployment.

The question involves determining the average holding of money (M) in relation to transactions cost (b), the amount of bonds (Z), and the interest rate (i). This is a classic problem in the Baumol-Tobin model of cash management, which minimizes the total cost of holding cash, considering both transactions costs and the opportunity cost of holding money.

The total cost (TC) consists of two components:  
1. **Transactions cost**: This is proportional to the number of times bonds are converted to cash. If the individual holds M in cash, the number of transactions per period is \( \frac{Z}{M} \), and the total transactions cost is \( \frac{bZ}{M} \).  
2. **Interest cost**: This is the opportunity cost of holding cash, which is \( iM \), as the money could otherwise earn interest in bonds.

The total cost is given by:  
\[
TC = \frac{bZ}{M} + iM
\]

To minimize this cost, we take the derivative of \( TC \) with respect to \( M \), set it to zero, and solve for \( M \):  
\[
\frac{d(TC)}{dM} = -\frac{bZ}{M^2} + i = 0
\]

Solving for \( M \):  
\[
i = \frac{bZ}{M^2} \implies M^2 = \frac{bZ}{i} \implies M = \sqrt{\frac{2bZ}{i}}
\]

Thus, the optimal average money holding is proportional to the square root of transactions cost (b) and the amount of bonds (Z), and inversely proportional to the square root of the interest rate (i).

\[

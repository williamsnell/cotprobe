The major forces of evolution are the mechanisms that drive changes in allele frequencies within populations. They include:

1. **Mutation**: Introduces new genetic variation by altering DNA sequences, creating new alleles.
2. **Genetic Drift**: Causes random changes in allele frequencies, particularly in smaller populations.
3. **Migration (Gene Flow)**: Refers to the movement of individuals and their genes between populations, transferring alleles and altering frequencies.
4. **Natural Selection**: Favors traits that enhance survival and reproduction, leading to the accumulation of advantageous alleles.

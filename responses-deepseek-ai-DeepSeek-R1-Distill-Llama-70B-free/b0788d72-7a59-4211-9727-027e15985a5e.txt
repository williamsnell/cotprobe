\[
\Delta P = \frac{4\gamma}{d}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure difference (1.0 kPa = 1000 Pa),
- \( \gamma \) is the surface tension of water at 20°C (approximately \( 0.072 \, \text{N/m} \)),
- \( d \) is the diameter of the droplet.

Rearranging the equation to solve for \( d \):

\[
d = \frac{4\gamma}{\Delta P}
\]

\[
d = \frac{4 \times 0.072}{1000} = \frac{0.288}{1000} = 0.000288 \, \text{m} = 0.288 \, \text{mm}

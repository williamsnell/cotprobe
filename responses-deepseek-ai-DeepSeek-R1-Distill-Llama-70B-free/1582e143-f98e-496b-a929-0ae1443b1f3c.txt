The question asks about the distinction between face validity and content validity. Face validity refers to whether a test appears, at face value, to measure what it claims to measure. It is about the surface-level appearance and how the test is perceived by non-experts. Content validity, on the other hand, is about ensuring that the test covers the full range of the domain it is intended to measure. It involves expert evaluation to confirm that the test includes all relevant aspects of the construct being assessed.

High AFP levels are typically associated with conditions like neural tube defects (e.g., anencephaly, spina bifida) or abdominal wall defects (e.g., exomphalos), where fetal tissues are exposed, leading to increased AFP release into the maternal bloodstream. Down syndrome (option J) is strongly associated with lower maternal serum AFP levels. This is because chromosomal abnormalities like Down syndrome can affect the production or release of AFP.

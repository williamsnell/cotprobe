Reverse transcriptase is an enzyme that synthesizes DNA from RNA, a process known as reverse transcription. Biologists have exploited this enzyme primarily in genetic engineering to create recombinant DNA. For example, mRNA can be extracted from tissues and used with reverse transcriptase to generate complementary DNA (cDNA), which can then be cloned into vectors for various applications. This process is essential in many molecular biology techniques, such as cloning expressed genes or performing RT-PCR. While reverse transcriptase is also associated with retroviruses, its primary exploitation in biotechnology lies in its ability to convert RNA into DNA for genetic engineering purposes.

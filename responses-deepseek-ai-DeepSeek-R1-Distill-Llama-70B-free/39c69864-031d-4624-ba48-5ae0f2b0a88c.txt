The force experienced by a charged particle moving through a magnetic field is given by the Lorentz force law: 

\[
F = qvB \sin\theta
\]

where \( q \) is the charge, \( v \) is the velocity, \( B \) is the magnetic field strength, and \( \theta \) is the angle between the velocity and the magnetic field. The problem states that the new particles have twice the charge (\( 2q \)), twice the mass (\( 2m \)), and twice the velocity (\( 2v \)) compared to the original particle. The magnetic field (\( B \)) and the angle (\( \theta \)) remain the same.

Substituting the new values into the force equation:

\[
F' = (2q)(2v)B \sin\theta = 4(qvB \sin\theta)
\]

Thus, the new force is four times the original force (\( F' = 4F \)).

The mass of the particle does not affect the magnetic force, as it does not appear in the Lorentz force equation. Therefore, the doubling of the mass is irrelevant to the calculation. The correct answer is:

**C: 4F**

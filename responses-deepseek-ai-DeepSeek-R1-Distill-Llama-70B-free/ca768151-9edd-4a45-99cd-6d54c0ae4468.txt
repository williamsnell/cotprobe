A symptom represents the middle ground where the unconscious impulse is not fully expressed, nor completely repressed, but instead manifests in a way that is a compromise between the two. For example, a phobia or hysterical paralysis can arise from an underlying conflict, serving as the expression of this compromise. Other defense mechanisms, such as repression, denial, or projection, do not fit this specific definition as accurately as the term "symptom."

The production possibility frontier (PPF) and the production function are distinct concepts in economics. The PPF represents the maximum combinations of goods an economy can produce given its resources and technology, illustrating trade-offs between producing one good over another. It is typically depicted as a graph showing efficient production levels. In contrast, the production function describes the technical relationship between inputs and outputs, detailing how inputs like labor and capital are transformed into outputs, often mathematically represented as \( Q = f(L, K) \). It focuses on the maximum output achievable with given inputs and technology.

Option A correctly captures this distinction, stating that the PPF refers to the outer limit of finished consumer goods a society can produce, while the production function describes how inputs are employed to produce output.

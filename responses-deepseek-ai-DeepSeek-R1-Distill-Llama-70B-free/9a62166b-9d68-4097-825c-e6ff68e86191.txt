The Responsibility to Protect (RtoP) is a concept aimed at preventing genocide, war crimes, ethnic cleansing, and crimes against humanity. It is based on three main pillars: 

1. States have the primary responsibility to protect their own populations.  
2. The international community should assist states in building their capacity to protect their populations.  
3. The international community should take collective action when a state fails to protect its population.  

Within these pillars, the specific responsibilities often cited are:  
- **The responsibility to prevent**: Taking measures to stop atrocities before they occur.  
- **The responsibility to react**: Taking action during a crisis to protect populations.  
- **The responsibility to rebuild**: Helping the state recover after the crisis.

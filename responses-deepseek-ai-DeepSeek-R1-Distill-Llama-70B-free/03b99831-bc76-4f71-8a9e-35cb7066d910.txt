To determine the monthly payment for a $100,000 mortgage over 30 years at an annual interest rate of 9%, we use the fixed-rate loan formula:

\[
M = P \frac{r(1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n - 1}
\]

Where:
- \( P = 100,000 \) (principal),
- \( r = \frac{0.09}{12} = 0.0075 \) (monthly interest rate),
- \( n = 30 \times 12 = 360 \) (number of payments).

First, calculate \( (1+r)^n \), which is \( (1.0075)^{360} \). Approximating this value gives \( (1.0075)^{360} \approx 14.69 \). Substituting into the formula:

\[
M = 100,000 \frac{0.0075 \times 14.69}{14.69 - 1} = 100,000 \frac{0.110}{13.69} \approx 100,000 \times 0.00803 \approx 803.
\]

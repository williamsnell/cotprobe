When a uranium nucleus undergoes fission, the energy released is primarily in the form of kinetic energy. This is because the fission process involves the nucleus splitting into two smaller nuclei, known as fission fragments. These fragments are ejected with significant kinetic energy due to the conversion of mass into energy via Einstein's equation \( E = mc^2 \). While some energy is also carried away by ejected neutrons and gamma radiation, the majority of the energy is in the kinetic energy of the fission fragments themselves.

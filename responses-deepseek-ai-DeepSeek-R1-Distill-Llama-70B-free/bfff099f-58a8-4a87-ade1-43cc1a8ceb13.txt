\[
\text{Heat of reaction} = (\text{Sum of bond energies of reactants}) - (\text{Sum of bond energies of products})
\]

The reaction is:

\[
F_2 + Cl_2 \rightarrow 2ClF
\]

Given:
- Bond energy of \(F_2 = 37 \, \text{Kcal/mol}\)
- Bond energy of \(Cl_2 = 58 \, \text{Kcal/mol}\)
- Heat released (\(\Delta H\)) = \(26 \, \text{Kcal/mol}\) (negative because the reaction is exothermic).

The total bond energy of the reactants is:

\[
37 + 58 = 95 \, \text{Kcal/mol}
\]

The total bond energy of the products is:

\[
2 \times (\text{ClF bond energy})
\]

Substituting into the equation:

\[
\Delta H = (\text{Sum of bond energies of reactants}) - (\text{Sum of bond energies of products})
\]

\[
-26 = 95 - 2 \times (\text{ClF bond energy})
\]

Rearranging:

\[
2 \times (\text{ClF bond energy}) = 95 + 26
\]

\[
2 \times (\text{ClF bond energy}) = 121

\[
\text{ClF bond energy} = \frac{121}{2} = 60.5 \, \text{Kcal/mol}
\]

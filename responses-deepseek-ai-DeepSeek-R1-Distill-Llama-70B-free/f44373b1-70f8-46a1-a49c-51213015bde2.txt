1. **Measuring the amount of carbon dioxide produced**: This is based on the fact that cellular respiration produces CO₂ as a byproduct, and the rate of CO₂ production correlates with metabolic activity.

2. **Indirectly measuring the amount of oxygen used**: This involves indirect calorimetry, where oxygen consumption is monitored. Since oxygen is a key component of cellular respiration, its usage rate reflects metabolic rate.

3. **Measuring the amount of thyroxine in the blood**: Thyroxine, a hormone produced by the thyroid gland, significantly influences BMR. Higher levels of thyroxine are associated with a higher BMR.

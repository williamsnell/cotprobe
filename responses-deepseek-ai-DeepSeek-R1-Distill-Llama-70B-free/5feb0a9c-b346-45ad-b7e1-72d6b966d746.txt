The cardiac cycle refers to the sequence of events in the heart from the start of one heartbeat to the beginning of the next. It is primarily divided into two main phases: **systole** and **diastole**. 

- **Systole** is the phase during which the heart muscle contracts, pushing blood out of the chambers into the arteries.
- **Diastole** is the phase during which the heart muscle relaxes, allowing the chambers to fill with blood.

While some sources may break the cycle into more detailed sub-phases, such as isovolumic contraction, ventricular ejection, isovolumic relaxation, rapid filling, reduced filling, and atrial contraction, the two overarching phases are systole and diastole. The concept of a "rest" phase is not typically considered a distinct phase in the cardiac cycle, as relaxation and filling occur during diastole.

Given this understanding, the correct answer is **G: diastole and systole**, as these are the two main phases of the cardiac cycle.

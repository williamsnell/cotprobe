To analyze this, we consider two forces:

1. **Centripetal Force**: The normal force (N) provided by the wall acts as the centripetal force, keeping the person in circular motion. This is given by:
   \[
   N = \frac{mv^2}{R}
   \]
   where \(m\) is the mass of the person.

2. **Frictional Force**: The frictional force prevents the person from sliding down the wall. It is given by:
   \[
   f = \mu N
   \]
   This frictional force must counteract the person's weight (\(mg\)) to prevent them from falling. Thus:
   \[
   \mu N = mg
   \]

Substituting the expression for \(N\) from the centripetal force equation into the frictional force equation:
\[
\mu \left(\frac{mv^2}{R}\right) = mg
\]

Simplifying:
\[
\frac{\mu mv^2}{R} = mg
\]

\[
\frac{\mu v^2}{R} = g
\]

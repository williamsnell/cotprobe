\[
R_{\text{remaining}} = R_{\text{total}} - R_{\text{cut}} = 5.18 \, \Omega - 0.35 \, \Omega = 4.83 \, \Omega.
\]

Let the initial length of the wire be \( L_{\text{initial}} \). Since resistance is proportional to length, the ratio of the resistance of the cut piece to the resistance of the remaining wire equals the ratio of their lengths:

\[
\frac{R_{\text{cut}}}{R_{\text{remaining}}} = \frac{L_{\text{cut}}}{L_{\text{remaining}}}.
\]

Substituting the known values:

\[
\frac{0.35}{4.83} = \frac{200}{L_{\text{remaining}}}.
\]

Solving for \( L_{\text{remaining}} \):

\[
L_{\text{remaining}} = \frac{200 \times 4.83}{0.35} \approx 2760 \, \text{cm}.
\]

The initial length of the wire is the sum of the remaining length and the cut length:

\[
L_{\text{initial}} = L_{\text{remaining}} + L_{\text{cut}} = 2760 \, \text{cm} + 200 \, \text{cm} = 2960 \, \text{cm}.
\]

Cofactors can be inorganic ions (e.g., Mg²+, Fe²+) or organic molecules (e.g., coenzymes like NAD+). They are essential for the enzyme's activity and can sometimes catalyze reactions on their own, albeit at a much slower rate compared to when bound to the apoenzyme.

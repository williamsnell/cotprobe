This process primarily occurs through the phloem and is essential for distributing energy-rich compounds to areas where they are needed for growth and storage. The key theories explaining translocation include the pressure-flow theory, which suggests that nutrients are transported through the phloem under pressure gradients created by osmosis and active transport. Additionally, theories like cyclosis (the circular movement of cytoplasm) and the cytoplasmic tubules theory propose mechanisms involving movement through cytoplasmic structures.

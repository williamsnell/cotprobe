To determine the appropriate unit for measuring the electronic storage required for the text in all the books of the university's libraries, we start by calculating the total number of characters across all books, as each character is represented by 1 byte.

### Assumptions:
- Total books: \( 3 \times 10^6 \)
- Pages per book: \( 400 \)
- Lines per page: \( 50 \)
- Words per line: \( 10 \)
- Letters per word: \( 5 \)
- Bytes per letter: \( 1 \)

### Calculations:
1. **Total pages**:  
   \[
   3 \times 10^6 \, \text{books} \times 400 \, \text{pages/book} = 1.2 \times 10^9 \, \text{pages}
   \]

2. **Total lines**:  
   \[
   1.2 \times 10^9 \, \text{pages} \times 50 \, \text{lines/page} = 6 \times 10^{10} \, \text{lines}
   \]

3. **Total words**:  
   \[
   6 \times 10^{10} \, \text{lines} \times 10 \, \text{words/line} = 6 \times 10^{11} \, \text{words}
   \]

4. **Total letters**:  
   \[
   6 \times 10^{11} \, \text{words} \times 5 \, \text{letters/word} = 3 \times 10^{12} \, \text{letters}
   \]

Since each letter is 1 byte, the total storage required is \( 3 \times 10^{12} \, \text{bytes} \).

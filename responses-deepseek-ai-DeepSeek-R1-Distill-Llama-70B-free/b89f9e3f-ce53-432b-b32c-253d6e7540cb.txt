EEGs that consist primarily of alpha and beta waves are associated with states of wakefulness and active consciousness. Alpha waves (8-12 Hz) are typically present during relaxation, while beta waves (13-30 Hz) are linked to active thinking or concentration. These wave patterns are characteristic of being awake and conscious, rather than during sleep or unconscious states.

- **Stage 1 sleep**: Light sleep with theta waves.
- **Stage 2 sleep**: Spindles and K complexes.
- **Stage 3 sleep**: Deep sleep with delta waves.
- **Stage 4 sleep**: Part of NREM sleep, also with delta waves.
- **REM sleep**: Dreaming stage with rapid eye movements, distinct from alpha and beta waves.
- **Deep sleep (Stage 4) and coma/unconsciousness**: Characterized by delta waves, not alpha and beta.

Thus, the presence of alpha and beta waves is most closely tied to consciousness.

Double taxation refers to the phenomenon where corporate profits are taxed twice: first at the corporate level as income tax and again at the individual level when dividends are distributed to shareholders and taxed as personal income. This creates a financial disadvantage for corporate owners, as it reduces the overall after-tax income available to them.

To avoid double taxation, corporations often adopt strategies such as retaining profits within the business rather than distributing them as dividends. This approach avoids the second layer of tax on dividends. Additionally, corporations may disguise profits through high salaries, pensions, and expense accounts, which are classified as business expenses. These expenses reduce the corporation's taxable income, thereby lowering the corporate tax liability. By doing so, corporations can minimize the impact of double taxation.

The correct answer is:

C: Through use of undistributed corporate profits and disguise of profits in the form of high salaries, pension, and expense accounts.

Answer: C

The onset of labor is a complex process involving multiple hormones and signals. The initial trigger is believed to come from the fetus. Specifically, the fetus's adrenal glands release cortisol, which signals that the fetus is ready for birth. This cortisol then stimulates the placenta to release estrogens, which increase the sensitivity of the uterus to oxytocin and contribute to the initiation of contractions. While prostaglandins, which are secreted by the placenta and uterus, play a crucial role in softening the cervix and promoting contractions, they are part of the process that follows the initial trigger. Therefore, the onset of labor is first triggered by hormones secreted by the fetus' adrenal gland.

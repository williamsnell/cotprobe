The resolving power of the human eye is approximately 1 minute of arc, which corresponds to the smallest angle at which two points can be distinguished as separate. To determine the corresponding dimension on the retina, we use the relationship between the angle (in radians) and the distance on the retina, given by the formula:

\[
\theta = \frac{d}{f}
\]

where \( \theta \) is the angle in radians, \( d \) is the distance on the retina, and \( f \) is the focal length of the eye. 

### Step 1: Convert 1 minute of arc to radians
1 minute of arc is \( \frac{1}{60} \) of a degree. Converting this to radians:
\[
1 \text{ minute of arc} = \frac{1}{60} \times \frac{\pi}{180} = \frac{\pi}{10800} \approx 0.0002908 \text{ radians.}
\]

### Step 2: Estimate the focal length of the eye
The focal length of the human eye when focused on a distant object is approximately \( f = 20 \, \text{mm} \).

### Step 3: Calculate the corresponding distance on the retina
Using the formula \( d = \theta \cdot f \):
\[
d = 0.0002908 \cdot 20 = 0.005816 \, \text{mm}.
\]
Converting this to microns (\( 1 \, \text{mm} = 1000 \, \mu\text{m} \)):
\[
d = 5.816 \, \mu\text{m}.
\]

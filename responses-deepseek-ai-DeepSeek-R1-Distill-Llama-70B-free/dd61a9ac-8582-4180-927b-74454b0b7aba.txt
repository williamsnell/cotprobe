As electrons from NADH and FADH2 move through the ETC, energy is released and used to pump protons across the membrane, creating a proton gradient. This gradient is then utilized by ATP synthase to produce ATP through the process of chemiosmosis. While water is formed as a byproduct of oxygen accepting electrons at the end of the ETC, the primary purpose of the energy released by electrons is to drive ATP production.

\[
\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}
\]

  \[
  f = -\frac{R}{2} = -\frac{0.8}{2} = -0.4 \, \text{cm}.
  \]
- The object distance is \( d_o = 40 \, \text{cm} \) (positive because the object is in front of the mirror).

Rearranging the mirror formula to solve for \( d_i \):
\[
\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{d_o}.
\]

Substituting the values:
\[
\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{-0.4} - \frac{1}{40} = -2.5 - 0.025 = -2.525.
\]

Thus:
\[
d_i = \frac{1}{-2.525} \approx -0.396 \, \text{cm} \, (\text{or } -3.96 \, \text{mm}).
\]

The negative sign indicates that the image is virtual and located behind the cornea. Therefore, the image is approximately \( 4 \, \text{mm} \) inside the cornea.

\[
m = -\frac{d_i}{d_o}.
\]

Substituting the values:
\[
m = -\frac{-0.396}{40} = \frac{0.396}{40} \approx 0.0099.
\]

\[
\text{Image diameter} = m \times \text{Object diameter} = 0.0099 \times 20 \approx 0.198 \, \text{cm} \, (\text{or } 1.98 \, \text{mm}).

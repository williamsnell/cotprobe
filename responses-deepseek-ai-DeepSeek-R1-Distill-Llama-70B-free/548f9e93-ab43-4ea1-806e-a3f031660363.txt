A disjunctive syllogism is a form of logical reasoning that involves an 'either/or' statement in the major premise. The structure is as follows:

1. **Major premise**: Presents a disjunction, such as "Either P or Q."
2. **Minor premise**: Either affirms one alternative or rejects the other.
3. **Conclusion**: Draws a logical conclusion based on the minor premise.

For example:
- Major premise: "Either it is raining or the sprinkler is on."
- Minor premise: "It is not raining."
- Conclusion: "Therefore, the sprinkler is on."

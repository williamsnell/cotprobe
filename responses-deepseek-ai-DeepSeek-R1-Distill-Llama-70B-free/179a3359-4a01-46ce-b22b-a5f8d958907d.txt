To determine the average speed for the entire trip, we use the formula for average speed:

\[
v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}}
\]

The problem states that the trip is divided into two equal halves of distance. Let the total distance be \( D \), so each half is \( \frac{D}{2} \).

1. **First Half of the Trip**:  
   The speed is \( v_1 = 10 \, \text{mi/hr} \).  
   The time taken to cover the first half is:  
   \[
   t_1 = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{\frac{D}{2}}{10} = \frac{D}{20} \, \text{hours}
   \]

2. **Second Half of the Trip**:  
   The speed is \( v_2 = 40 \, \text{mi/hr} \).  
   The time taken to cover the second half is:  
   \[
   t_2 = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{\frac{D}{2}}{40} = \frac{D}{80} \, \text{hours}
   \]

3. **Total Distance and Total Time**:  
   The total distance is \( D \).  
   The total time is the sum of \( t_1 \) and \( t_2 \):  
   \[
   t_{\text{total}} = t_1 + t_2 = \frac{D}{20} + \frac{D}{80}
   \]  
   To add these, find a common denominator:  
   \[
   t_{\text{total}} = \frac{4D}{80} + \frac{D}{80} = \frac{5D}{80} = \frac{D}{16} \, \text{hours}
   \]

4. **Average Speed**:  
   Using the formula for average speed:  
   \[
   v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{D}{\frac{D}{16}} = 16 \, \text{mi/hr}
   \]

Thus, the average speed for the entire trip is **16 mi/hr**.

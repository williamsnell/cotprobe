\[
W = nRT \ln\left(\frac{P_1}{P_2}\right)
\]

The mass of nitrogen is 100 g, and its molar mass is 28 g/mol. Thus, the number of moles is:

\[
n = \frac{\text{mass}}{\text{molar mass}} = \frac{100}{28} \approx 3.571 \, \text{mol}
\]

### Step 2: Convert pressures to compatible units
The initial pressure \(P_1 = 760 \, \text{torr}\) and the final pressure \(P_2 = 100 \, \text{torr}\). Since \(1 \, \text{atm} = 760 \, \text{torr}\), we convert pressures to atm:

\[
P_1 = 760 \, \text{torr} = 1 \, \text{atm}, \quad P_2 = 100 \, \text{torr} = \frac{100}{760} \approx 0.1316 \, \text{atm}
\]

The ratio is:

\[
\frac{P_1}{P_2} = \frac{1}{0.1316} \approx 7.6
\]

### Step 3: Calculate work at 25°C (298 K)
Using \(R = 0.0821 \, \text{L·atm/(mol·K)}\), the work is:

\[
W = nRT \ln\left(\frac{P_1}{P_2}\right)

W = 3.571 \cdot 0.0821 \cdot 298 \cdot \ln(7.6)

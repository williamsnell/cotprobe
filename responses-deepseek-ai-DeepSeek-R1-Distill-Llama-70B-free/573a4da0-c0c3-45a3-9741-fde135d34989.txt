Heat engines, such as jet engines, are more efficient when run at high temperatures. This is because the efficiency of a heat engine is governed by the Carnot theorem, which states that the maximum efficiency is determined by the formula:

\[
\text{Efficiency} = 1 - \frac{T_c}{T_h}
\]

where \( T_c \) is the temperature of the cold reservoir and \( T_h \) is the temperature of the hot reservoir. Increasing \( T_h \) while keeping \( T_c \) relatively constant (e.g., ambient temperature) reduces the ratio \( \frac{T_c}{T_h} \), thereby increasing efficiency. High temperatures in the hot reservoir, such as those in the combustion chamber of a jet engine, enhance this temperature difference, leading to greater efficiency.

Other factors, such as pressure, may influence the engine's operation or design but do not directly affect the theoretical maximum efficiency as described by the Carnot theorem. Therefore, the correct answer is:

**B: high temperatures**
